# Color Revolutions

**Author**: Paul Spencer (Zeitville Media)
**Date**: January 18, 2026
**Source**: zeitvillemedia.substack.com

---

It's an all hands on deck moment. A dazzling display of colors is about to smash the status quo.

Many will find this exciting. Others will find it frightening. Beauty can be terrorizing when you've lived in a monochrome hell for too long.

People adopting divergent perspectives—"this is good change, this is bad change"—will be at the heart of late January's trends and narratives.

Do you feel the icebreaker is good, like all the rigid patterns needed shattering anyways?

Or were you comfortable in the stagnation, knowing that it wasn't stability but you could pretend anyways?

You could say that 2026 really begins now. Not on January 1st. Right here, at this Capricorn New Moon.

We've been laboring under an intense bracelet of planets in Capricorn. Specifically, all the personal visible planets that affect daily life and 3D events: Sun, Mercury, Venus, Mars.

In fact, almost all of these are crammed together at the very end of Capricorn—then over this week, one by one, they will spill into radical Aquarius.

Capricorn New Moons are about consistency. And what we're going to get is a consistent string of revolutions. Expect regime changes and volatile market conditions.

But also expect relationship changes.

## Venus Leading the Pack

You see, just in the nick of time, Venus jumped into Aquarius—leading the pack and telling us that partnerships are where the revolution happens first.

Earlier in the month she was in Capricorn with her lover boy Mars, influencing the collective to prioritize slowness in relationships. But when there's too much Capricorn like this, consistency turns to stagnation. We did not see passion, we saw concern and even fear and hoarding—the dense shadow side of Capricorn.

Instead of a sexy, creative power couple dynamic in business and love, what I noticed everywhere was that people felt constricted. Yes, it brought a lot of discernment, scrutiny, and kicking tires on what really serves us going into 2026. But how it felt was pretty low energy. Not very vital.

There was too much dry logic, almost cruelty in the air. We saw shocking events in Minnesota and Iran.

Thus, here are the archetypes and storylines you should keep an eye out for now:

- Sentiments of rejection, rebellion, refusal, revolution
- Shocking surprises that catch people off guard
- Heretics, outcasts, outliers and fringe artists
- An attack on once trusted institutions
- Surges of terror as old forms evaporate

## Icebreaker

This New Moon at the tail end of Capricorn signals the true beginning of 2026.

The message is a question, as most New Moons pose a question.

Do we go toward the Patchwork Age—creating texture, mystery, and awe by making collages from the rubble of Big Tent society?

Or do we go into the Glasslands, where everything has been nuked into a smooth, convenient, infinite streaming landscape? DoorDash, tap-to-pay, grind it out on the dating apps. The place where nothing happens but distraction reigns supreme.

Why is this forking path so intense right now? Because there's not much Capricorn energy for us to draw on. Pluto entered this sign in 2008, laying waste to the capstones of society, like tradition and collective ritual and civility.

All the top-down hierarchies have been destroyed, even though people cling to the illusion that they're still around (or can come back). That illusion began creeping in around 2011, when Neptune entered Pisces. We thought smartphones could create a parallel utopia, much like the early 90s World Wide Web.

And when Uranus entered Taurus in 2018, we thought apps could take that utopia even further.

But both of these planets are at the end of their very long visits at this New Moon. Saturn has been in Pisces since March 2023, exposing our lies and self-sabotage in society. He is also about to leave Pisces in a couple weeks with Neptune.

Pisces rules pandemics, large scale politics and religion, art, illness, mental health. It's like we've collectively been on a long fentanyl bender.

Can you say culmination point? This is a changing of the guard if I ever saw one.

It's like a panic moment in the collective boardroom. "Where can we possibly go from here?"

Pressure. Choices. Let's keep going.

## Smashing Glass

We're seeing destabilization, regimes falling, revolutions heightened by all these cycles culminating and ending. Pluto is fresh in Aquarius, having finished his long stay in Capricorn after the 2024 elections. He's been priming us for this moment.

So as planets spill across him in the days after this New Moon, we'll refuse the lack of color in our world.

We're smashing outdated self-talk paradigms, carrot-stick models and reward-punishment systems.

Aquarius won't tolerate old identities produced by nightmare factories posing as dream factories—it rejects your false reality and builds parallel ecosystems.

Aquarius lives in the hypothetical, in fractal options, in pivoting. It believes in the future.

So we're refusing old paradigms that punish us. We're done begging for morsels of belonging to something that doesn't exist anymore—the Big Tent society of shared culture.

Now it's fragmented into hyper-niches, reality tunnels, micro-tribes. But some may look at this and decide to plug into the Glasslands, numbed.

Globally, it's forked path urging us to responsibly and honestly assess institutions and paradigms. Does this one-size-fits-all serve me, or am I squeezing into a box just to belong to some phantom oasis of security?

The Aquarius mantra is: I don't care about acceptance, I care about alignment with my vibration, I hypothesize outside the given scripts.

The ice is breaking. We're not taking it anymore.

Because what's the alternative if you want to be normal and status quo?

Not knowing how to flow forward will lead to freak outs or back to Big Tent cruelty.

Here lies the temptation of the Glasslands: infinite streaming, monoliths gobbling up everything, a frictionless society where you see humans but don't connect with them, all mystery and texture are managed by smooth faceless entities.

Yet if you choose the Patchwork Age, you get: multiplicity, parallel realities, combinations. You're a goth tearing it up on Friday nights, a finance bro during the day, barefoot gardening on Saturdays, tinkering with crypto on Tuesday.

Recent headlines are already revealing these storylines. Let's take a look.

## Color of the Year

This recent viral graphic of home design colors really says it all. In the place of contrast through multiple colors, we've descended into a homogenous Glassland.

We've also see this happen with pop songs. Key changes in top hits are becoming scarce.

All of this primes 2026 to be a breakout year with color returning. The revolutionary moment is now, as this Capricorn New Moon will be followed up by a vibrant Leo Full Moon and then an Aquarius New Moon Eclipse on February 17. (This is also the Chinese Lunar New Year, a Fire Horse Year.)

Get ready for creative chaos. Again, the first place you'll see this hit is relationships. People will push for more connection, authenticity and individuality.

A thoughtfluencer you may be familiar with, who goes by Signulll on X, recently encouraged people to 'optimize for a new modernity' by refusing dinner dates as first date ideas and finding ways to make a quick escape if necessary. Andrew Cote replied that "utilitarian calculus is the antithesis of passion."

This debate aligns with another recent take of Signulll's that I pushed back on, where he claimed that people will increasingly do things not because they desire to do them but because it aligns with the profile or brand they're trying to project.

"Optimizing for new modernity" is not going to fly at all in 2026 and I think you'll see the seeds of that now. People don't want to be another number.

It's not really a tech vs anti-tech thing. It strikes me that rational thoughtfluencers are woefully unprepared for the emerging zeitgeist because they are in love with their models that calculate societal outcomes rather than accommodating "creative unknowing" indicated and supported by cosmic or spiritual cycles.

There could be many reasons people refused to not see these cycles. If you read my previous newsletter, I think it ultimately comes down to the relationship these individuals have with their birth family and work families. At root, there is identification with a reward/punishment system.

"If I get love, it's because I did something to deserve it. If something bad happens to someone else, it's because they had it coming and didn't follow the model."

That's all crashing down now—and indeed we could see people crashing out as reality outpaces their precious little zero sum models.

So until we meet again on the February 1st Leo Full Moon, I ask you to ponder a choice:

Do you want a technicolor patchwork world—or do you wish to wander the monochrome Glasslands?
