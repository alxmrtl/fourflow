Box Design Proposals

Replacing the generic 3px top bar. Each proposal shown across all 4 pillars.
The new key layout (framing question + key container with icon) is shown in all cards.

Proposal A

"Pillar Slash" — Corner Chamfer

The top-left corner is geometrically cut (chamfered), revealing the pillar color as a structural notch — like a keyhole or a deck of cards fanned out. No bar, no shadow. Just a precise architectural cut. The colored corner signals which pillar without needing a label.

What's unique: No other major design system does this. It's angular in a sea of rounded cards, signals the pillar through geometry rather than decoration, and scales perfectly — the chamfer is the brand mark.

Proposal B

"Signal Pulse" — Left Spine

A thin vertical colored line on the left edge carries 3 equally-spaced circular nodes — one per Flow Key in the pillar. The active key's node glows full color; others rest at dim. No top bar. The card edge becomes a living mini-legend.

What's unique: The dots are functional, not decorative — they mirror the 3 keys inside the card. Clicking a key makes a dot glow. Most distinctive on dark bg; communicates "system" without any labels.

Proposal C

"Frequency Line" — Animated Wave

A subtle SVG sine wave runs across the top edge in the pillar color — like an EEG readout, a sound wave, a signal trace. At rest it breathes slowly. When a key is active, it flows continuously. Flow as frequency — directly on-brand.

What's unique: No other wellness/productivity brand uses this. The wave is both visual metaphor (flow = frequency) and subtle animation — it doesn't shout but it moves. Immediately distinctive on scroll.

Proposal D

"Glyph Corner" — Pillar Sigil

No bar. No line. A small branded sigil (◈) sits in the top-right corner of every card, glowing softly in the pillar color. Like a wax seal or a classification mark — it says "this belongs to a system." Premium, editorial, quiet.

What's unique: Maximum restraint. The card is almost barebones — the sigil is the only decoration. Reads as ultra-premium. Works well if the content itself is strong enough to carry the card (which it is).

Proposal E

"Interference Grid" — Dot Mesh

The top strip of each card shows a tight dot-grid pattern in the pillar color — like a signal broadcast, a circuit board header, or an oscilloscope. The mesh fades into the dark card background. Quietly technical, distinct, patterned.

What's unique: Textural rather than linear. Every other design in this space uses lines — this uses a field. Suggests "data," "signal density," "something beneath the surface." Works especially well for the /me page context.
