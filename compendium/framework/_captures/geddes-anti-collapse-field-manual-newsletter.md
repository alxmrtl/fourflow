# The Anti-Collapse Field Manual

> **Author**: Martin Geddes · **Type**: newsletter

## Core Argument
Most failures are not failures of will or knowledge — they are failures under load. When pressure rises, systems terminate questions at the first level that can carry the load. The intervention: identify the load-bearing function of any behaviour, replace (don't remove) it, and rebuild upward from stable lower layers. You cannot skip layers under pressure.

## Mechanics Enriched

- [[challenge-skill-calibration]] — Load-Capacity Diagnostic: adds the load dimension to challenge-skill calibration; if load > capacity, descent is automatic
- [[anxiety-suppression]] — Under-Load Entry Protocol: 5-second triage ("what problem is being solved?" + "where has the question stopped?")
- [[error-tolerance]] — Load-Bearing Behaviour Reframe: persistent failures are load-bearing structures; replace function, don't fight habit
- [[psychological-safety]] — Stabilisation Sequence: stability precedes clarity, rebuild upward in order, never force L4 with unstable lower layers
- [[commitment-escalation]] — Pseudo-Covenant Detection: resolution without structure collapses under pressure; "never again" is not covenant
- [[identity-alignment]] — Covenant vs. Authority Audit: L1 (authority/feels right) vs L3 (covenant/identity); most alignment failures are L1 mistaken for L3
- [[internal-alignment]] — Operative Worship Test: worship is not what is professed but what is not subject to revision; absent vs simulated vs operative
- [[cognitive-load-reduction]] — Termination Level Awareness: cognitive overload causes premature resolution; principle → rule → justification → impulse

## Standout Quotes

> "You're not breaking. You're stabilising badly."

> "If a behaviour persists under pressure, it is carrying load."

> "Don't remove a behaviour without replacing its function."

> "Pseudo-covenant = resolution without structure. If it must be re-decided under pressure, it is not covenant."

> "Worship is not what is professed. It is what is not subject to revision."

> "Stability precedes clarity. Grounding precedes enforcement."
