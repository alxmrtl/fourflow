# Your brain works against you until you give it a meaningful goal

> **Author**: unknown · **Type**: x-post · **Context**: reply thread citing Dan Koe

## Core Argument

The brain doesn't idle without goals — it defaults to the Default Mode Network, which burns the same energy but directs it inward toward rumination and anxiety. A specific, meaningful goal triggers PFC takeover, dopamine reward loops, and DMN suppression — reorganizing the entire neural system toward directed effort. Purpose extends this reorganization across time, with measurable physiological effects including dramatically lower mortality.
