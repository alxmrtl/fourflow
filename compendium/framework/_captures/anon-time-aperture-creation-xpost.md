# What Time Actually Is (When Creation Is Already Finished)

> **Author**: Unknown · **Type**: x-post

## Core Argument

Time is not a feature of reality but a feature of the lens — the frame rate of a finite aperture sweeping across an infinite, already-complete structure. Flow widens the aperture (hours vanish); anxiety narrows it (seconds drag). The delay between internal shift and external reflection is the buffer where faith is tested. Time is something you are doing, not something happening to you.
