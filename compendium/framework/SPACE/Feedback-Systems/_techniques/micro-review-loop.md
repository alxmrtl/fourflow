# Micro-Review Loop

> Two minutes at the end of each work unit closes the loop while the context is still live — end-of-day review closes it after the thread has been cut.

## Protocol
1. Define your work units in advance — the natural boundaries where you will pause. For writing: paragraphs or sections. For coding: functions or feature increments. For strategic work: decision points or meeting-driven transitions. The unit should be the smallest chunk that constitutes a meaningful completion.
2. When you reach a unit boundary, stop fully. Do not immediately begin the next unit. Two minutes minimum.
3. Ask three questions in sequence: (a) What did I actually produce? (Name it concretely — not "I worked on the section" but "I drafted the argument for X.") (b) Was there a moment of resistance or unexpected ease? (c) If I started this unit again, what would I do differently in the first 30 seconds?
4. Write the answer to question (c) only. One sentence. This is your capture. Do not write summaries or long reflections — just the one actionable observation.
5. Begin the next unit. The captured observation feeds into how you approach it.
6. At end of session, review all captures from that session. They form a diagnostic trace of where friction lived.

## When to Use
- Any work session where craft improvement is a goal, not just output production
- During a new phase of work where you are operating at the edge of current competence — when mistakes are most informative
- When you notice a pattern of finishing sessions feeling uncertain about quality but unable to specify why

## Key Insight
> The feedback you need is available right now — in two minutes — but will be mostly gone in two hours.
