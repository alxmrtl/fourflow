# Honest
*The Restore quality of Feedback Systems*

## What it is

When this quality is present, the signals you receive about your work tell the truth. You can look at the feedback and know whether something moved. When absent, the loop has become distorted — by self-serving interpretation, by measuring the wrong thing, or by feedback so delayed and diluted that cause-effect has gone opaque. You can be busy and producing output and still have no reliable signal about whether any of it is working. That uncertainty is where motivation quietly unravels.

## Science

The experience of personal causation — the felt sense that your actions are producing perceptible consequences — is one of the primary drivers of intrinsic motivation. Deci and Ryan's self-determination theory identifies competence (the experience of effective action) as a core psychological need; when feedback distorts or hides this evidence, the competence signal degrades and intrinsic engagement follows.

Self-serving bias compounds the problem. Research on performance attribution shows that people systematically misattribute outcomes: successes to internal factors, failures to external ones. Without deliberate external calibration — a comparison baseline, a peer reference, an objective metric — self-assessment drifts toward distortion. The feedback loop appears intact but is no longer honest.

The restore path isn't more feedback — it's cleaner feedback. Signal-to-noise calibration: separating actionable information from irrelevant variance, anchoring baselines so progress reads accurately, and using external reference points to correct for the systematic distortions built into self-perception. Honest feedback doesn't need to be frequent or elaborate; it needs to accurately reflect whether the work is moving.

## Recall

**Hook**: You can't own what you can't see — visible cause-effect is the foundation of motivation.
**Mechanism**: When actions have perceptible consequences, the brain treats effort as meaningful; without honest agency loops, effort feels disconnected from outcome and attention disengages.
**Anchor**: Agency = seeing what your actions caused. No loop = no ownership.
