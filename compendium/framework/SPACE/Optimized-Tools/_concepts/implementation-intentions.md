# Implementation Intentions

> Peter Gollwitzer's finding that if-then planning ("If X happens, then I will do Y") dramatically increases follow-through on intentions compared to simple goal-setting — by pre-deciding the response and removing the decision cost from the moment of action.

## What It Is

Implementation intentions were introduced by Peter Gollwitzer (1999) as a distinct form of planning that specifies the when, where, and how of goal-directed action in advance. While goal intentions specify what you want to achieve ("I intend to work deeply this morning"), implementation intentions specify the triggering condition and the precise response ("If it is 9am and I sit at my desk, then I will immediately open the document and begin writing"). The if-then structure creates a contingency plan that fires automatically when the trigger is encountered.

Gollwitzer's meta-analysis of 94 studies found that implementation intentions roughly doubled the rate of follow-through on intentions, with effect sizes (d ≈ 0.65) substantially larger than simple goal-setting or motivational interventions. The mechanism is automaticity: by pre-deciding the response, the person removes the in-the-moment decision process — the moment the trigger occurs, the action fires without deliberation. The trigger-action link is encoded as an automatic association, functioning more like a habit than a decision.

Research by Aarts, Dijksterhuis, and others confirmed the neurological basis: implementation intentions create strong associative links between situational cues and behavioral responses, reducing the need for central executive involvement at the moment of action. Pre-decided tool use is a direct application: when you have a rule ("If I'm starting a writing session, I open this specific app and nothing else"), the decision friction at session start is eliminated before it can drain willpower.

## Why It Enables Flow

The transition from "about to start" to "in flow" requires navigating a decision bottleneck: which tool, which document, which approach, which task. Each undecided element at session start is a micro-cost on willpower and a delay on momentum. Implementation intentions eliminate this bottleneck by transforming decisions into automatic responses — the trigger (sitting down, opening the computer, a specific time) fires the pre-decided action chain without deliberation.

Frictionless entry to deep work is not about motivation — it is about removing the decision layer from the path to starting. Implementation intentions are the cognitive architecture for accomplishing this: pre-decided tool selection, pre-decided workspace setup, pre-decided first action.

## Application

For each regular deep work context, write an explicit if-then plan: "If [trigger condition], then I will [specific first action with specific tool]." The more specific and concrete the trigger and response, the stronger the automaticity. Over 2-4 weeks of consistent execution, the if-then pair becomes habitual — the trigger fires the action without deliberation.
