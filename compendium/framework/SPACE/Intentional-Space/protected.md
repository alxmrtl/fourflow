# Protected
*The Maintain quality of Intentional Space*

## What it is

When this quality is present, the session has room to build. Nothing breaks the continuity before flow can establish itself. When absent, that accumulation never happens — not because the work is wrong or the motivation insufficient, but because the structural minimum for flow (uninterrupted attention across 15–25 minutes) keeps getting reset before it's reached. A session without interruption protection isn't a distracted session. It's a session that was structurally incapable of producing flow from the start.

## Science

Gloria Mark, Daniela Gudith, and Ulrich Klocke (2008) found that the average knowledge worker takes 23.15 minutes to return to the original task after an interruption — not because the interrupting event was long, but because re-establishing context requires suppressing attention residue from both the prior task and the interrupting content. Sophie Leroy's (2009) attention residue research explains the mechanism: switching to a new context — even briefly — generates active background processing of the prior task that persists into the new one. An interruption creates residue in both directions simultaneously.

Flow's accumulation requirement compounds this. Csikszentmihalyi's research identified a minimum of 15–20 minutes of uninterrupted focus as the structural precondition for flow onset. A single intrusion resets that accumulation. A session with frequent interruptions isn't eight interrupted flow states — it is a sequence of flow attempts that never reach onset.

In aperture terms, each interruption forces the aperture to a different focal point and resets the process. Protecting the session is therefore not a comfort preference; it is the minimum structural condition for flow to emerge at all.

## Recall

**Hook**: An interruption doesn't pause flow — it destroys it. The cost is counted in the 23 minutes before depth returns.
**Mechanism**: Flow requires uninterrupted accumulation time to emerge; each interruption resets that accumulation; attention residue from the interruption persists even after the external event ends — fragmenting both the prior and new context.
**Anchor**: Protect the session before it starts. Notifications off, door closed, expectations set. The interruption-gate-protocol makes this systematic.
