# Environmental Trigger Stack

> The nervous system responds to conditioned sequences — stack enough channels and flow-entry becomes a reflex.

## Protocol
1. Identify five sensory channels to work with: visual (light quality, what you see), auditory (background sound, silence, specific music), olfactory (scent — coffee, specific fragrance, fresh air), proprioceptive (seating position, desk height, specific physical posture), and tactile (specific tool in hand — pen, keyboard, particular notebook).
2. Choose one specific, consistent stimulus for each channel that you will associate exclusively with deep focus sessions. Exclusivity is critical: if the stimulus appears in non-focus contexts, the conditioning weakens. The scent you use for focus sessions should not be present during non-focus time. The specific music or sound profile should be used only for focus.
3. Assemble the stack as a defined sequence: first visual (light on/adjusted), then olfactory (scent activated), then proprioceptive (posture set), then auditory (sound on), then tactile (tool in hand). The sequence itself becomes a conditioned cue — the brain begins transitioning to the focus state during the sequence, not only after it completes.
4. Apply the full stack at the beginning of every focus session for at least three weeks. Consistency during the conditioning phase is required — partial stacks during this phase dilute the conditioning.
5. After conditioning is established (typically 3-6 weeks of consistent use), the stack will generate a noticeable state-shift. At this point you can use abbreviated versions (2-3 elements) when the full stack is not available, and the partial pattern will still activate the associated state.
6. Audit the stack seasonally — some elements naturally change (scent preferences, sound environment). Update deliberately rather than letting the stack decay through inconsistency.

## When to Use
- When building a new dedicated workspace or significantly redesigning an existing one
- When the warm-up phase of focus sessions is consistently long and resistance-heavy
- When you need reliable focus access in multiple different physical locations — portable elements of the stack travel with you

## Key Insight
> You are not trying to feel like focusing — you are training the environment to generate that feeling for you.
