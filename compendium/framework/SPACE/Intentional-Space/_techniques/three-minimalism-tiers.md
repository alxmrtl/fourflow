# Three Minimalism Tiers

> A framework for calibrating *how much* to simplify based on performance goals and life circumstances — not as a moral judgment but as a conscious strategic choice.

## Mechanism
Environmental simplicity isn't binary. The relevant question isn't "should I be a minimalist?" but "which level of simplicity is optimal given my current goals, situation, and trade-offs I'm willing to make?" This framework makes the choice explicit, preventing the vague aspiration to "simplify" without an actionable target. Each tier has a different filtering question and a different attention-cost profile; knowing which tier you're targeting makes acquisition decisions and purge decisions concrete.

## The Three Tiers

**Tier 1 — Aggressive Minimalism**
- **Filter**: "Is this a tool that directly advances my craft?" Yes = keep. No = cut. No exceptions.
- **What it looks like**: Entire life portable; mornings seamless with no possessions requiring management; every item has a specific functional purpose in current work.
- **Best suited for**: Periods of intense professional focus; high mobility; deep commitment sprints.
- **Trade-off**: Social friction, limited comfort, difficulty if circumstances change.

**Tier 2 — Tempered Minimalism**
- **Filter**: "Does this serve comfort or flow — and when they conflict, does flow win?"
- **What it looks like**: Small, efficiently organized home; multi-functional furniture; a few well-loved pieces; no accumulation; high-quality essentials.
- **Best suited for**: Settled knowledge workers who want lasting high performance without extreme sacrifice. The most sustainable target tier for most people.
- **Trade-off**: Requires ongoing discipline; things must leave when things enter.

**Tier 3 — Mild Minimalism**
- **Filter**: "Did I choose this intentionally, knowing the attention cost?"
- **What it looks like**: Owns more than essentials (family, hobbies, art, personal meaning) but with conscious intention. Aware of the trade-off without eliminating possessions that aren't purely functional.
- **Best suited for**: People with family commitments, established homes, or meaningful non-work possessions — who want conscious relationship with their environment without extreme simplification.
- **Trade-off**: Higher baseline cognitive load; requires active awareness to prevent drift.

## Protocol

1. Identify your current tier (honestly assess what you own and why).
2. Identify your target tier based on current life circumstances and performance goals.
3. If target is lower than current: schedule a [[possession-purge-protocol]] to recalibrate.
4. Use the target tier's filtering question for all future acquisitions and annual audits.
5. Revisit the tier choice annually or when major life circumstances change (relocation, career shift, family change).

## Key Insight
> "You don't need to be a monk — you need to know which tier you're at and own the trade-off."
