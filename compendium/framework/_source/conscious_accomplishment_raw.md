## Working with Your Response to Life

One concept is about working with your response to life. This will be important to juxtapose with the rest of the framework - whether it's in the self dimension or as the awareness of all four dimensions.

**Toying with an idea**: There might be a 13th mystery element that encompasses all of them. Maybe we would call it SOUL instead of self - that which observes and provides insights to all these different elements or keys as part of the human OS.

### Resistance and Triggers

A big concept in working with your life is becoming conscious of resistance. These manifest as triggers. Often we have a conditional sense of well-being - how we feel is determined by whether certain conditions are fulfilled, like having your preferences met.

**Outside-In Paradigm**: The person thinks their problems and solutions exist in the world outside of them. At the core, this is a strategy to feel good.

**Inside-Out Paradigm**: The book argues for the opposite - address inner resistances to what is happening now. Resistance to life is when you oppose or avoid life unfolding exactly as it is.

**Symptom of resistance**: When circumstances around you take you out of a calm, relaxed state - a state highly amenable to flow.

The book calls these **disturbances**. In the context of flow and the framework, we should have an all-encompassing word for this - disruptor, disturbance, or friction. This friction might happen at various levels of the dimensions.

**Alternative approach to well-being**: Problems aren't objective circumstances but rather how we relate and react to them.

**Experiment**: Note triggers. Bring awareness to the things that bring us out of the calm, relaxed state. Put them in a notebook.

## Talking to Our Heart

When we're not in flow, we interact with life with consciousness patterns (beliefs or reactions). These are fixed ways of dealing with information we perceive in the outside world - like instruction sets for our perceptions and behaviors that allow us to react without needing to process.

**Consciousness patterns** are usually the root cause of why we get disturbed. They make unconditional well-being (and finding flow) difficult.

**Imprints** are the initial and ongoing exposure events that create and inform consciousness patterns.

Until we work on our consciousness, most of the way we interpret the world is through our patterns. For many people looking for flow, the first step is taking care of these patterns so they can be integrated more easily. This opens up the flow of information and insight from our higher self or soul.

## Awareness

We can talk about awareness of true reality as a spotlight. Awareness can spotlight a small part of life, but as our awareness grows, we can see more possibilities.

Taking initiative to expand your awareness is like working toward accessing a more powerful spotlight. This process expands our capacity to see greater truth about ourselves and the world.

**Key definitions**:
- **Awareness**: The ability to see true reality clearly
- **True reality**: The ultimate unchanging truth underlying our everyday material existence
- **Expand awareness**: To see a greater portion of true reality
- **Realization**: The process of becoming aware of an existing truth (synonymous with recognition and understanding)

It's common to mistake current understanding for true reality instead of recognizing it as partial understanding.

Our actions and approach to life are determined by our depth of awareness and realization. The more we see true reality clearly, the more we can align perspective and actions.

**What expanded awareness reveals**:
- Why we are the way we are
- Which subconscious influences impact our thoughts, emotions, and behaviors
- What beliefs artificially constrain our experience
- The purpose we have in life and how we can uniquely contribute
- The deeper essence of what we are
- The guiding principles of how the universe works

Awareness seems like our lens from the soul perspective - a 13th key that observes all four dimensions interacting at once.

## Consciousness as an Ecosystem

Consciousness functions as part of a dynamic ecosystem informed by:
1. **Reference system**: Innate functions, stored patterns
2. **Energetic heart**: A latent center of perception that informs emotional experience
3. **True self**: The eternal part of our being, unified with the source of all creation

The primary way to restore access to the true self is through opening the heart.

Thoughts, emotions, perceptions, and behaviors we experience are determined by the configuration and functioning of these ecosystem contributors.

### Two States

**Disintegrated consciousness ecosystem**: Connections to true self and heart are largely offline, causing consciousness to function through patterns we're identified with.

**Unified consciousness ecosystem**: Consciousness, heart, and true self function as one. In this state, you experience the qualities of an open heart and wisdom of your true self within your awareness.

*Need to figure out the best way of translating these terms to existing keys and dimensions.*

### More on True Self

The true self is eternal and the most essential part of our being. It's the naked awareness that can perceive information completely, which we can use to navigate life. Recognized by many wisdom traditions.

The true self is with us every moment - we can lose contact but never actually lose it. Its perspective and capacity is quite different from most people's normal awareness. It exists beyond time and space, allowing it to see the totality of the universe and have unlimited capacity to access wisdom.

This positions it well as the SOUL aspect in the expanded framework.

### The Energetic Heart

Its function is feeling emotions. The output is experienced in conscious awareness. The quality of emotional experience depends on the state of the heart.

**Factory setting**: Joy of an open heart. When open, it manifests natural qualities:
- Trust
- Openness
- Love
- Gratitude
- Deep reverence for the wisdom of the universe

These natural qualities are innate to the heart - like an open mind, we want an open heart.

The heart loses power and natural qualities when conditioned by unhealthy patterns. Patterns are like chords that make opening the heart difficult and cause it to close quickly. Without knowing it, our patterns train the heart to remain closed to keep us safe.

**Conditional functioning**: When something good happens, heart opens (positive feeling - but fleeting). When something bad happens, heart stays closed (challenging thoughts and emotions).

## Trust

The state of trust is a natural quality when the heart is open - a natural state. Most of us don't feel this way because patterns in our reference system say otherwise, causing conditional functioning.

**Reactions indicating lack of trust and closed heart**:
- Anxiety, nervousness, second-guessing
- Seeking maximal certainty, needing reassurance
- Needing control, procrastination
- Unwarranted cautiousness, being in a hurry
- Needing to figure it out, needing to know
- Perfectionism, trying to do it right
- Neuroticism about any part of life
- Being tormented by loss and gain
- Avoiding and hiding, sense of scarcity
- Clinging, unfounded meaning-making
- Self-doubt

Anytime we notice these reactions, we're likely responding from patterns instead of openhearted trust.

**First step**: Strengthen the sense of trust first, then cultivate other open-heart qualities (openness, love, gratitude, deep respect for life).

## The Virtuous Cycle

Working with response to life creates a virtuous cycle that expands awareness:

1. Meet and transform unhealthy patterns
2. Less disturbed → more flow can happen
3. Improved capacity to observe activities in awareness
4. See more (previously eluded things): when heart closes, unhelpful thought loops, patterns, how reality functions
5. Quieter mind → enhanced pattern recognition → awareness expands

It's not that we no longer experience challenging thoughts or emotions - they just become less frequent.

**Summary**: Work toward unifying consciousness ecosystem by:
1. Noticing when heart is closed and patterns causing it
2. Noticing when consciousness is disturbed to transform unhealthy patterns
3. Increasingly resting in spacious awareness to facilitate refined capacity for 1 and 2

Possessing innate sense of trust = stable internally generated confidence everything will work out.

Absence of open heart qualities indicates pattern causing heart to close. Use instances of closed heart as entry point to become aware of why and transform underlying pattern.

Heal and purify unhealthy patterns by:
- Facing them directly
- Replacing them with new information aligned with true self and true nature
- Continuously shifting consciousness patterns toward alignment with true nature

This restores heart to natural open state. Working on patterns creates conditions for clear/open mind - supports ongoing awareness and transformation.

## Re-Patterning: An Inherited Operating System

We have patterns underneath our reactions, usually buried in subconscious. Until we do the inner work, we don't know what pattern is driving how we relate to reality - we just feel the weight of the world.

All patterns based on survival reinforce the idea we're not safe → causes heart to close or function conditionally.

**Re-patterning is key** to fundamentally shift how we experience the world.

### High-Level Process

Identifying → Facing → Accepting → Replacing

Once identified, face it, accept it, replace with new information. The replacement step is critical - provides instructions for how you want information and energy in reference system to change.

We don't want no patterns - we want patterns that reflect our true nature. Rely on wisdom of true self for replacement, or use open heart qualities if that's not available.

**Pattern replacement examples**:
- Self-doubt → deep trust in life unfolding
- Trying to control → openness to life's intelligence
- Judgment → love
- Self-pity → gratitude

## Consciousness Re-Patterning Instructions

The high-level sequence (similar to alchemical practices): Identify → Face → Accept → Replace

More specific sequence: **Identify → Access → Welcome → Accept → Replace → Embrace**

### Approaching with Supportive State

Two important components:
1. **Perform in calm, relaxed state** - the most important input for effective transformation. Use breath sequence if needed.
2. **Make the goal simply to meet what's there** rather than aggressive agenda to change. Approach with curiosity (link to curiosity key and open mind).

### Step 1: IDENTIFY

Recognition happens throughout day by noticing when disturbed. A disturbance can be overt triggering or subtle (thoughts taking you out of peace). Reacting indicates meeting reality from unhealthy pattern instead of open heart/true self.

Often when disturbed, you're in the middle of something - note the experience in your freedom log and do the full practice later.

### Step 2: ACCESS

Goal: Connect with information and energy in subconscious related to the pattern or resistance.

This step happens later in the day in calm seated position with eyes closed.

**Calming breath**: Breathe in slowly (I), out slowly (am), in slowly (here), out slowly (now).

**Set intention** to connect with heart and true self. Shift attention to heart space in chest. Connect through intention and feeling its natural qualities.

Once heart connection felt, invite connection to true self through intention. No perfect way to do this. Tell true self you're open to receiving its wisdom.

Aim to enter practice feeling heart, true self, and consciousness all unified.

From this state, mentally recall the circumstance/feeling/reaction that disturbed you. Can prompt verbally with phrase about how you felt.

**Three questions** (any order):
- What does this pattern feel like? (emotions)
- What thoughts are associated? (words, ideas, pictures, memories)
- What physical sensations? (body response)

These map nicely to the three self keys.

### Step 3: WELCOME

As you recall and ask questions, goal is to welcome whatever emerges and allow it to be there. No need to prevent, change, or create meaning. Welcoming is more posture than discrete action.

The welcoming process is supported by relaxing into your experience.

Though patterns are composed of thoughts, emotions, physical sensations, and behaviors - apply a label to it. Trust whatever feels natural. The mere process of observing is doing the work.

**Example labels**: "I'm not good enough," "I'm scared," "I think I need to do everything right," "I'm sad"

Discovering patterns is like finding next layer of sediment blocking the majesty of the temple within.

### Step 4: ACCEPT

If welcoming = surveying everything in your basement, accepting = acknowledging what you've discovered.

Goal: Shift relationship with inner material from subconscious obscurity to acceptance. Creates conditions for transformation.

Acceptance ≠ continuing to live with it or resigning to it. Also ≠ identifying with it. Just fully acknowledging.

**Initiate with question**: "Can you accept that you [pattern]?"
- "Can you accept that you thought you're not good enough?"
- "Can you accept that you feel scared?"
- "Can you accept that you think you need to do everything right?"

Don't intellectually answer - keep attention on what emerges in present moment. True self delivers information in emergent fashion. Get direct indication of yes or no.

If you can't accept or don't want to - two moves:
1. Ask "What's underneath?" → unveils adjacent or deeper pattern
2. Accept that you can't accept something

### Step 5: REPLACE

Goal: Transform unsupportive inner material into quality aligned with true nature.

All previous steps = finding a secret program in your operating system. Now it's about programming with new instructions.

Use innate ability of consciousness to direct information and energy, or allow true self to do it on your behalf.

**With true self connection**: Maintain attention on heart, ask "What would you like to replace this with?" Notice what emerges (feeling, voice, sensation, image, knowing, or all). Remain open to inner wisdom.

**With conscious mind**: Use open heart qualities as replacements:
- Unconditional trust
- Unconditional openness
- Unconditional love
- Unconditional gratitude
- Unconditional respect for wisdom of universe

Example: Replacing self-doubt → actively generate feeling of unconditional trust by imagining what it feels like and allowing.

### Step 6: EMBRACE

Solidify and reinforce by fully experiencing. Embracing = fully experiencing something and allowing it to be there.

Relax into updated feelings, let them wash over you. Not forceful process.

Example: If replacement is unconditional love, shouldn't feel like concentration. More like allowing presence of love to expand.

Can help to visualize being enveloped in the replacement. Relax and rest as long as you can or see fit.

Carry this feeling with you through the day.

**To work on additional patterns**: Simply restart the process.

### Summary

- Goal: Take information/energy no longer supportive → transform into healthier qualities aligned with true nature
- Continuous effective re-patterning shifts reference system composition → creates conditions for heart to open
- Relies on consciousness to shift information while gradually integrating wisdom of heart and true self
- Patterns often have many layers → may encounter new information in practice → triggers may repeat
- This is expected; keep working the process

## Pattern Literacy

The book presents 10 fundamental patterns that undermine well-being, wisdom, and realization (access to natural open heart state):

1. **Polarized thinking**: Black/white, good/bad, right/wrong
2. **Over-generalization**: Jumping to conclusions without considering totality
3. **Unfounded suspicion**: Over-emphasizing negative potentialities without indication
4. **Negativity/pessimism**: Assuming the worst
5. **Fixed expectations**: Believing things should be a certain way (usually your preferences)
6. **Greediness**: Striving for more than you need
7. **Self-centeredness**: Over-prioritizing yourself unhealthily
8. **Avoiding and hiding**: Orchestrating life to avoid facing things
9. **Stubbornness**: Clinging to your position
10. **Fight for dominance/competitiveness**: Zero-sum thinking, needing to put others down

**Extended unhealthy patterns list**:
- Approval seeking, arrogance, being noncommittal
- Being calculating, tormented by loss and gain
- Believing it's all up to you, blame and victimhood
- Clinging and resisting change, comparison
- Competitiveness, criticism, defensiveness
- Desire to be special or important
- Disturbed by interference or interruption
- Eagerness for quick results, entitlement
- Excessive restraint or inflexibility
- Expecting the world or others to supply you
- Feeling like running out of time
- Feeling threatened irrationally
- Holding extreme expectations, hostility
- Hypocrisy and double standards, indecisiveness
- Jealousy, judgment, lack of trust or doubt
- Manipulation, militancy
- Means-to-end thinking or behaviors
- Needing to always produce or be productive
- Needing certainty or needing to know
- Obligation, over-accommodation
- Perfectionism or always needing to do it right
- Pride, rebellion or rejecting authority
- Retribution or getting even
- Righteousness or need to be right
- Scarcity, self-abandonment, self-doubt
- Self-judgment, selfishness, skepticism
- Shirking responsibility or blame
- Spiritual drivenness or obsessiveness
- Spiritual bypass
- Thinking you're above certain experiences
- Striving for prestige
- Suppression or avoidance
- Trying to control, trying to figure it out
- Trying to escape core human conditions (death, challenging emotions)

**Key reminder**: Just because something is socially normal doesn't mean it aligns with your deeper nature or unconditional well-being.

Developing intellectual understanding of unhealthy patterns makes it easier to notice and work with them. Many "normal" patterns reflect cultural conditioning more than true nature.

**Practice**: Pick one or two to focus on initially, then gradually expand once easily recognized.

### Scope and Subtlety

The continuum of working with response to life contains:

**Scope** - types of reactions you're aware of:
- External triggers
- Inner disturbances
- Subtle impulses
- Perceptual frames
- Whether heart is open or closed

**Subtlety** - how refined your ability to notice is within each type.

With greater awareness, you can increase scope and subtlety. Because awareness increases both, life can feel MORE disturbing initially. This usually reflects increased ability to see patterns that were always present.

## Paradigm Shift and Transformational Conflicts

Prioritizing consciousness creates transformational conflicts - tension between emerging perspectives and current circumstances.

Healing patterns and suppressed material supports emergence of more authentic desires and preferences.

Being attached = strong emotional/mental/physical bond where presence or outcome affects well-being.

**Navigate transformational conflicts**: Take any resistance into re-patterning.

### Inner Purgatory

An intermediate state between focusing on inner work and feeling invigorated for creative action in the world.

Reignite fire for creative action by unblocking whatever's in the way in your reference system.

**Watch for polarized thinking**: Often prevents enjoying previous sources of meaning from updated perspective.

**Content vs Context**:
- Content = surface level reality of what's happening
- Context = motives, reasons, circumstances around action
- Context is often more telling than content

Resisting being in transformational process can prolong inner purgatory. Once you accept the process, you can move through more graciously.

## Acceptance and Surrender

**Acceptance**: Acknowledging the reality of a situation
**Surrender**: Relinquishing the need to control the outcome

Acceptance and surrender ≠ resignation or abandoning agency. They deal with how you relate to the present moment.

You can accept and surrender to reactions, then take action to improve circumstances. You can accept reality as it is AND feel inspired to make it better.

## Inspired Action vs. Means-to-End Action

**Inspired actions**: Motivated by intrinsic reward
**Means-to-end actions**: Motivated by desire to get something outside yourself you think will make you feel complete

Most means-to-end actions driven by unhealthy patterns (low self-esteem, doubt, lack of safety).

**Common pattern blocking inspired action**: Lack of trust for following your inspiration.

**Create conditions for inspired action**: Work on patterns that obstruct it. These will present themselves in how you currently relate to following your inspiration.

## Conscious Accomplishment

The process of working toward aspirations to inform opportunities for self-awareness and transformation.

Primary practices:
- Working with response to life
- Re-patterning

**Ultimate goal**: Unconditional well-being, fulfillment, and supporting your contribution to the world.

**The cycle**: Taking action toward aspirations ↔ Working with response to life

*Very similar to flow cycle*: Struggle phase (working with response to life) → Letting go → Flow

### Mountain of Success vs. Spiral of Fulfillment

**Success**: Achievement of desired outcomes
**Fulfillment**: Deep and lasting sense of satisfaction, contentment, completeness

How you define success is highly influenced by conditioning.

**Conscious accomplishment prioritizes fulfillment over success** while recognizing you can have both.

Greatest fulfillment comes from continuously developing greater awareness, realization, and wisdom.

## Ambition: Clean Fuel vs. Dirty Fuel

Context of ambition determines whether it supports or undermines consciousness evolution.

**Healthy ambition**: Motivated by qualities aligned with true nature (love, inspiration, desire to contribute)

**Unhealthy ambition**: Actions emanating from unhealthy patterns created by ego wanting to avoid harm. Means-to-end actions often fueled by this.

**Determine context of ambition by**:
- Examining where you feel pushed vs. pulled
- Working with response to life
- Asking yourself why you want things

Healthy ambition usually results in better outcomes long-term because it's easier to sustain activities and energy.

## How to Relate to Desire

*This might map to curiosity key*

Conscious accomplishment encourages accepting and embracing authentic desires - supports greater awareness.

Your consciousness evolution is less about whether desires meet some spiritual ideal and more about using desires to see yourself clearly.

Full acceptance of desires allows unencumbered movement and greater agency.

Denial of desires creates inefficiency, unsatisfactory results, frustration, and undermines self-awareness.

**Goals are useful** for creating direction. What matters for spiritual growth is how you relate to them.

*May relate to worthy mission or vision as well*

Attachments = expecting reality to be a certain way (in flow context, blocks creativity and going with the flow).

**Process**: Moving toward aspirations to become aware of attachments

**Capital-L Letting Go**: Being OK in the deepest part of your being with whatever happens. This is true surrender - much more powerful than intellectually letting go.

*Potential thread*: High levels of emotional triggering in first flow cycle step → finding ability to let go of resistance/patterns that block flow. Possible link between intensity of emotion and intensity of flow available.

## Life's Intelligence

**Life's intelligence**: The underlying force that animates and orchestrates life.

The more you understand how it functions, the more you can align with it for positive outcomes.

### Two Reality Views

**Linear reality**: Life directed by straightforward cause and effect

**Responsive reality**: Acknowledges influence of underlying intelligence and interconnectedness between inner and outer worlds

Expanded awareness creates conditions to realize life's intelligence, which seeks:
- Your continuous evolution
- Your authenticity
- Your contribution to the whole

Life's intelligence spurs personal evolution by orchestrating circumstances that aid awareness and transformation.

### Working with Life's Intelligence

It behaves to encourage:
1. Your consciousness evolution
2. Internal integrity
3. Following its natural direction

These support growth, authenticity, and contribution.

**Working with response to life** = natural way to use opportunities life's intelligence brings.

**Internal integrity** = whether aligned with emotions, sensations, and authentic desires in inner space. Life's intelligence responds to your internal integrity to move you toward deeper authenticity.

**Natural direction**: Life's intelligence has direction it wants things to go - supports your personal evolution and development of the whole. Discern by noticing ease or friction as you move toward things.

### Cultivating Trust

Supported by:
- Understanding how life's intelligence functions
- Removing blocks to natural trusting state
- Accruing evidence

**Strengthen foundation**:
- Continuously work with situations where you're not trusting
- Progressively bold experimentation

Trust in life's unfolding occurs on a continuum aligned with depth of awareness. The more you realize how life's intelligence functions and recognize its benevolent nature, the easier to trust unconditionally.

**If stressed about figuring out life's direction**: Indicates lack of trust. Most productive thing is to use this circumstance for inner work.

**If you miss guidance**: Life's intelligence will continue to deliver opportunities.

**Expanding trust supported by realizing**:
- You can't see the totality of things
- Challenges spur your evolution
- Life's intelligence may know your deeper desires better than your conditioning does
