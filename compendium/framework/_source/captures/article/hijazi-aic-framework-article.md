# To Know or To Do: Timing & Awareness-Intent-Creativeness (AIC)

## Main Question

When should you acquire knowledge versus take action? This tension between knowing and doing forms the core inquiry of Ahmad Hijazi's framework presented in *The Atlas of Worldly Wisdom* (TAWW).

## The Central Framework: Awareness-Intent-Creativeness (AIC)

The AIC triple helix describes three interconnected flows:

1. **Awareness** - gaining knowledge about the world
2. **Intent** - planning next moves
3. **Creativeness** - acting to shape circumstances and lives

According to Hijazi, "worldly wisdom lies in perfecting & timing the flows of life."

## Three Core Problems

### Awareness: Fruitless Knowledge

Knowledge that doesn't translate into actionable understanding carries limited practical value. The framework emphasizes learning about "the boundaries of current knowledge" rather than endless information gathering. Incomplete knowledge proves particularly dangerous because it creates illusions of understanding.

### Creativeness: Aimless Action

Taking action without comprehending broader context wastes effort. The analogy offered: "a great navigator using a wrong map" or "power applied in the wrong context just breaks things." Action requires precision and focus paired with flexible environmental awareness.

### Intent: Bad Strategy

Intent oversees the integration of knowledge and action. The PARCS model (Present-Actions-Resources-Controls-Success) provides a strategic framework addressing this challenge.

## The Solution: Timing and Courage

Hijazi proposes: "Have the courage to take *some* risk, once you know *enough*" where:

- **Some** = acceptably accurate risk estimation
- **Enough** = the point where acquiring additional knowledge becomes prohibitively costly

This acknowledges that perfect information remains impossible; subjectivity determines acceptable risk thresholds based on personal objectives.

## Definitions Within TAWW

- **Awareness**: integrating knowledge into perceptive, actionable form
- **Intent**: visualizing needed change and accepting associated risks
- **Creativeness**: bringing something valuable into existence through action

## Fractal Structure

These spirals aren't truly separate. Each contains aspects of all three, constantly flowing between knowledge absorption, creation, and planning—reflecting how "our adventures are fractals."
