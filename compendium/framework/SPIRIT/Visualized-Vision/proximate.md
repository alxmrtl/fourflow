# Proximate
*The Maintain quality of Visualized Vision*

## What it is

When this quality is present, the session belongs to a larger arc. What happens today is part of what's being built over months and years — and the brain's reward system knows it, which means each coherent action generates a reward signal rather than requiring a fresh justification. When absent, short actions are islands: Monday's work doesn't connect to Tuesday's, the brain treats each day as a separate optimization problem, and the orienting cost is paid fresh every morning. Long-horizon coherence is not about working toward an abstract distant goal — it's about making the connection between today's action and that goal vivid enough to be motivationally present in the session.

## Science

Goal proximity research (Kivetz, Urminsky & Zheng, 2006) demonstrates that motivation increases as the goal feels closer and more concrete — the goal-gradient effect. Long-horizon coherence creates a version of this effect across time: by making the connection between present action and distant outcome explicit and vivid, it makes the distant goal motivationally proximate even when it's months away. The 90-day vivid scene technique operationalizes this — a 90-day horizon is far enough to be meaningful and close enough to feel real.

Temporal self-appraisal (Ross & Wilson, 2002) shows that people evaluate their past decisions through the lens of their current self-concept, which means present-day coherence shapes how the past is remembered — creating a reinforcing narrative of consistent direction. Practitioners with high long-horizon coherence don't just have a better future; they remember their past more coherently, which makes the present action feel like a natural continuation.

The compound growth mechanism: consistent directional choices navigate through a branching possibility space with increasing specificity. The brain registers each coherent action as progress on a long-range attractor, sustaining motivation across sessions. Incoherent choices — switching direction in response to noise rather than signal — circle the same territory without building the momentum that carries into genuinely new outcomes.

## Recall

**Hook**: Coherence across time is how you reach the good outcomes. Every consistent directional choice is a fork taken toward the same region.
**Mechanism**: Each coherent action registers as progress on a long-range attractor, generating reward; incoherent choices circle the same territory; the brain sustains motivation toward goals it can perceive as proximate — long-horizon coherence creates that proximity.
**Anchor**: Monthly Trajectory Review — plot the last 30 days; is the line straight, gently curving, or zig-zagging? For zig-zags: signal (real feedback worth integrating) or noise (doubt, fear, comparison)? Signal refines; noise scatters.
