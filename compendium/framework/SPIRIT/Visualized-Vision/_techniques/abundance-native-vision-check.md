# Abundance-Native Vision Check

> Test whether your vision is organized around what you're called toward (abundance-native) or what you're fleeing from (scarcity-derived) — a critical distinction for whether the vision creates clean pull or a mixed threat-signal that never fully resolves.

## Protocol

1. State your current vision clearly.
2. **Primary test**: "Is this vision pulling me toward something genuinely new, or pulling me away from something I fear?" Toward = abundance-native. Away-from = scarcity-derived.
3. **Abundance diagnostic**: Ask: "If all material scarcity were removed tomorrow — no financial pressure, no status to protect, no competition to win — what would I do?" What you can honestly answer to this question is closer to your abundance-native vision than almost anything else.
4. **Mouse Utopia check**: Does the vision optimize for stimulation, comfort, and relief from stress? (Technically satisfying but no genuine vocation, no genuine challenge, no genuine contribution.) If yes — this may be scarcity-derived even if comfortable, organized around never being lacking again rather than genuine calling.
5. **Kingdom check**: Is the vision organized around authentic vocation, genuine love, and contribution that compounds across time? Does it involve discomfort — not as suffering to endure, but as the natural friction of doing something that matters?

**What to do with scarcity-derived visions**: Don't discard them — they carry real information about what you fear and what you genuinely need. Work on the underlying pattern that makes the scarcity feel threatening. What remains after that work is closer to the abundance-native core.

## When to Use
After completing any vision-building exercise (especially the anti-vision contrast primer). When a vision feels motivationally compelling but accompanied by a persistent anxious quality. Before committing significant time and energy to a directional trajectory.

## Key Insight
> "A vision built on scarcity premises pulls you toward the goal and away from fear at the same time — that dual orientation never fully resolves, and flow can't emerge in that state."
