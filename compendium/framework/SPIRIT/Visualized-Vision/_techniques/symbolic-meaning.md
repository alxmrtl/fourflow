# Symbolic Meaning

Find the single image, word, or metaphor that most immediately activates the feeling of your vision — then use that symbol at session transitions instead of re-explaining the vision analytically.

## Protocol

1. Write out your vision in full analytical form first — where you're going, what it looks like, why it matters.
2. Read it and notice which phrase, image, or word most immediately activates the feeling of it. The one that makes something in you say "yes." That's your symbol candidate.
3. Test it: use or visualize the symbol alone, without the surrounding explanation. Does the feeling activate quickly? If yes, that's your working symbol.
4. If no symbol emerges, try drawing the vision instead of writing it. Visual encoding often surfaces symbols that verbal encoding doesn't.
5. Use the symbol at session entry (especially on low-motivation days), at session transitions, and at moments of discouragement. The symbol activates the state in seconds; the analytical explanation takes minutes.

*Review the symbol every 90 days — as the vision develops, the symbol may need updating. Does it still activate the feeling? If not, find a new one.*

## When to Use

- At the start of sessions when motivation needs activating and the full vision explanation would take too long
- When discouragement appears mid-session and you need rapid reconnection to purpose
- When building a daily practice of vision anchoring

## Key Insight

> "A symbol activates the state in a second. An explanation takes a minute. Use the symbol during work."
