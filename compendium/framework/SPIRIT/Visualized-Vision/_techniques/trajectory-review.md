# Trajectory Review

> Monthly audit of major decisions and commitments to assess whether action is coherent over time — distinguishing signal-based course corrections from noise-based coherence leaks.

## Mechanism
Every consistent directional choice is a fork taken toward the same region of possibility. Coherent choices navigate you genuinely somewhere new; incoherent choices (left, right, left) leave you circling the same area. Motivation doesn't decay when each session registers as progress on a long-range attractor — but without trajectory visibility, coherence leaks happen invisibly. The review makes the trajectory visible so it can be maintained or deliberately updated.

## Protocol

1. Monthly, list the major decisions, focuses, and commitments of the last 30 days. Be specific: what did you actually spend time on, what did you commit to, what did you say no to?
2. Draw a line through them. Is it:
   - **Straight**: coherent direction, sustained trajectory
   - **Gently curving**: evolving coherently as you learn more
   - **Zig-zagging**: incoherent; multiple direction changes without clear reason
3. For zig-zags: identify what caused each direction change. Was it:
   - **Signal** (new information, genuine feedback from reality, learning that changed your understanding) → healthy; refines trajectory
   - **Noise** (doubt, comparison with others, fear, impatience with the mirror) → coherence leak; scatters direction
4. Signal-based corrections are healthy and should be integrated. Noise-based corrections should be reversed or acknowledged as errors.
5. From this clarity: recommit to the current direction, or make a deliberate course correction with full clarity about why.

## When to Use
Monthly, scheduled in advance as a recurring event — not triggered by feeling lost (which happens after coherence has already degraded). Also useful before major decisions that could alter trajectory.

## Key Insight
> "You're not choosing your future. You're choosing your trajectory through a structure that already contains every future. Coherence across time is how you reach the good ones."
