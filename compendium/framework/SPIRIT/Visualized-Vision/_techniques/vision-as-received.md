# Vision as Received

> Shift relationship to your vision from effortful climbing toward it to receptive clearing — you are the conduit through which the vision enters, not the climber striving to reach it.

## Mechanism

Most people relate to their vision as a summit to climb: something far away that must be earned through sufficient effort, proof, and preparation. This frame activates efforting and makes the gap between present reality and future vision the primary felt experience. The alternative: the vision already exists in a realm of potentiality and is selecting you as the specific person through whom it can manifest. It has been seeking a conduit — you are the doorway. The job is not to achieve the vision but to clear the conditions that block it from arriving. This reframe dissolves the effort-worthiness dynamic (I must work hard enough to deserve it) and replaces it with a receptivity dynamic (I must remove what's blocking it). Practically, what blocks it: attachment to the how, conditional self-worth, and ignoring the excitement that IS the vision's navigation signal.

## Protocol

**Relationship shift practice** (when the vision feels distant or heavy):
1. **Notice the climbing frame** — are you relating to the vision as something you're striving toward? What's the feeling? Usually strain, insufficiency, or the sense that you're behind
2. **Invert the direction** — the vision is descending toward you, not the reverse. You are standing still; it is approaching. Feel the difference in the body
3. **Ask: what's on the landing pad?** — what internal conditions are creating interference? Common blocks: attachment to a specific how, earned-worth beliefs, excitement you're not following, anxiety about deserving it
4. **Clear one block** — use the relevant technique for whatever is on the landing pad (radical-allowance for how-attachment, worthiness-as-baseline for self-worth, excitement-as-GPS for unexpressed excitement)
5. **Return to the vision** — prime it briefly (30–60 seconds of felt contact with it), then release and act

**Conduit orientation** (ongoing):
When working on anything connected to the vision, periodically ask: "Am I climbing, or am I being moved?" The climber has tight shoulders and counts steps. The conduit is relaxed, responsive, and trusts that the movement is happening. Adjust accordingly.

## When to Use

- When the vision feels like a burden or obligation rather than a pull
- When you're measuring how far you still have to go (climbing frame) instead of clearing blocks
- When effort is high and the vision still feels distant despite sustained work
- When the vision has lost its luminosity — this often means the relationship has shifted to striving
- During vision priming sessions as an alternative orientation to goal-oriented visualization

## Key Insight

> "You're not climbing toward the vision. The vision is descending toward you, and you just need to clear the landing pad. Your job isn't to achieve the vision — it's to allow the vision to achieve itself through you."
