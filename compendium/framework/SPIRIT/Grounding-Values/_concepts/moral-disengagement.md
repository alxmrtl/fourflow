# Moral Disengagement

> Albert Bandura's theory of how people bypass their own moral standards — understanding the mechanisms of disengagement is the foundation of deliberate re-engagement.

## What It Is

Moral disengagement, developed by Albert Bandura in the 1990s, describes the cognitive mechanisms by which people selectively disengage their internal moral self-regulatory standards to act incongruently with their values without experiencing guilt or self-censure. Bandura identified eight distinct mechanisms: moral justification (reframing harmful behavior as serving a higher purpose), euphemistic labeling (sanitizing language to make harmful actions less salient), advantageous comparison (making the behavior seem acceptable relative to worse alternatives), displacement of responsibility (attributing action to authority), diffusion of responsibility (spreading culpability across groups), dehumanization, attribution of blame (framing victims as deserving), and disregard or distortion of consequences.

What makes moral disengagement particularly important is that it is not a failure of values — people who engage in these processes typically hold genuine values. Rather, disengagement is a failure of the link between values and behavior: the self-regulatory mechanism that would normally trigger discomfort when values are violated is selectively switched off. The process is largely automatic and operates below conscious awareness, which is why values-behavior gaps can persist even in people with strong stated commitments.

## Why It Matters for Grounding Values

Congruent practice is, mechanistically, the deliberate reversal of moral disengagement — re-engaging the self-regulatory processes that Bandura identified as deactivatable. Each act of naming a rationalization, refusing euphemistic framing, or accepting rather than displacing responsibility is an act of moral re-engagement. The Integrity Audit technique maps directly to Bandura's taxonomy: it asks the practitioner to scan for the specific cognitive mechanisms being used to create separation between values and behavior.

Understanding disengagement also shifts the framing from willpower to awareness. Values-behavior gaps are not usually caused by insufficient commitment — they are caused by sophisticated cognitive justifications that feel entirely reasonable from inside. The congruent quality works not by adding more force, but by recognizing the disengagement mechanism the moment it activates.

## Application

- Audit rationalizations using Bandura's eight categories — identify which mechanism is in operation when values and behavior diverge
- Notice euphemistic language as an early signal of disengagement before the behavior occurs
- Practice attributing responsibility concretely to yourself when diffusion or displacement mechanisms activate
- Use the Integrity Audit to make disengagement visible, not to generate guilt but to restore self-regulatory contact with values
- Recognize that moral disengagement is normative — the goal is not its elimination but its early detection
