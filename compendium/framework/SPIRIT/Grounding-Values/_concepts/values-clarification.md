# Values Clarification

> The process of identifying genuine core values — not aspirational ideals that sound good, but the actual principles revealed under pressure and conflict when you must choose between competing goods.

## What It Is

Values clarification is a structured inquiry into what actually matters most to you — distinguished from what you think should matter, what you've been told to value, or what sounds admirable when listed. The distinction is critical: aspirational values are what you'd like to stand for; genuine values are what you actually stand for, revealed by the choices you make when it's costly.

The pressure test: genuine values are revealed not by what you say in calm moments but by what you sacrifice for. If you claim to value deep work but consistently choose reactive communication when both are possible, reactive communication is closer to your actual value. If you claim to value family but consistently choose work when they conflict, work is closer to the actual value. This is not a moral judgment — it's a diagnostic.

The clarification process involves two steps: (1) generating a candidate list of values and (2) rank-ordering them under conflict. The ranking reveals priority, which is where the real information lives.

## Why It Enables Flow

Flow requires internal consistency. Work that is mis-attributed to values you don't actually hold generates the values-behavior gap — a persistent low-level dissonance that drains energy and prevents full commitment to the task. When you know your actual values and structure your work accordingly, the values layer supports rather than resists the work.

Additionally: clarified values simplify decisions. When you know what matters most, the answer to "what should I do now?" is often obvious. Decision overhead — one of the primary sources of cognitive load — collapses.

## Application

The sorting exercise: list 10-15 values. Then repeatedly ask: "If I had to choose between these two, which would I choose?" Force-rank them. The ranked list is your actual values hierarchy — often different from the aspirational list. Structure your work and time allocation around the top 3-5.
