# Internal Integrity Check

> A pre/during-work check for whether actions match authentic desires — distinguishing the productive friction of growth from the draining friction of misalignment.

## Mechanism
Internal integrity is the degree to which your external actions match your internal truth. When high, the whole system moves in one direction and energy flows toward the work. When low, part of your energy goes toward suppressing the misalignment — a silent tax on every session that the brain must pay before any focused work begins.

## Protocol

**Before significant work**, ask: "Am I aligned with what I'm doing, or am I overriding something?"

**High-integrity signals:**
- Actions feel natural, even when challenging
- No suppressed voice saying "this isn't right"
- Energy flows toward the work rather than managing discomfort
- Outcomes matter less because the process itself feels aligned

**Low-integrity signals:**
- Persistent tension or resistance during action
- Rationalizing why you're doing something
- Energy leaking into suppression, performance, or forcing
- Needing outcomes to justify the misalignment

**The key distinction**: this check is not "does this feel easy?" — growth involves challenge. The question is whether the challenge comes from stretching toward something real, or from betraying your truth. Ease within effort signals alignment; persistent friction despite effort signals misalignment.

**During long work periods**: check in at natural transitions. If the signal changes mid-session, note it without immediately stopping — complete the natural unit, then assess.

## When to Use
Before beginning any significant project, session, or commitment. At natural transitions within long work periods. When motivation is low and it's unclear whether the issue is internal resistance or genuine misalignment.

## Key Insight
> "When aligned, the whole system moves together — mind, heart, body, and deeper self oriented toward the same direction. When misaligned, you're fighting yourself while trying to work."
