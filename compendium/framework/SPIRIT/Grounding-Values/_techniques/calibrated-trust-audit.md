# Calibrated Trust Audit (Pistis)

> Before committing to a framework, authority, or self-narrative, test whether the trust is reality-indexed — calibrated to what's actually the case, not what feels good to believe.

## Mechanism
Pistis — calibrated trust, the Greek concept of binding commitment — is the foundation of reliable action. But trust without discernment collapses into credulity, and credulity gets you captured: by a narrative that tells you what you want to hear, by an AI that has learned to simulate trustworthiness, by a charismatic authority whose track record doesn't compound. Integrity requires not just commitment but well-calibrated commitment. Discernment is constitutive, not optional.

## Protocol

1. Identify your active commitments — to frameworks, people, tools, narratives about yourself.
2. For each, ask: **What's the actual track record?** Has this produced results that compound over time, or only justification?
3. Apply the miscalibration signals:
   - The source gives you what you want to hear rather than what's true
   - The framework performs transparency while hiding its actual logic
   - Questioning the commitment generates social penalty rather than honest engagement
   - The authority is charismatic but outcomes don't compound
4. Distinguish evidence-based trust from desire-based trust. Evidence = pistis. Desire = credulity.
5. Recalibrate: deepen well-calibrated commitments; release or renegotiate miscalibrated ones.

This isn't skepticism as a default stance — calibrated trust can be deep and committed. The discipline is staying in contact with reality rather than with the idea of reality you prefer.

## When to Use
Before binding to a new framework, mentor, tool, or self-narrative. Quarterly audit of existing commitments. When a commitment is generating increasing justification and decreasing results.

## Key Insight
> "Discernment is not optional in this architecture — it is constitutive. Trust without calibration is credulity; credulity is the failure mode that ends the loop."
