# Ignited Curiosity

> The pull force in the flow equation — genuine interest that draws you toward a domain and sustains engagement through difficulty. With it, flow is the natural result of following where attention leads. Without it, flow requires willpower to enter.

## Why It Enables Flow

Curiosity and flow share the same entry mechanism: both are activated by a gap between what you know and what you want to know. Curiosity creates intrinsic pull toward the challenge — which means the struggle phase feels interesting rather than threatening, and the next step is always visible (follow the question). When curiosity is absent, every difficult moment requires a volitional push. When it's present, the work carries you.

The failure mode is extrinsic orientation: working for outcomes (money, status, validation) rather than genuine process interest. The problem isn't that people lack curiosity — it's that they've buried it under layers of "should" and "practical." The work of Ignited Curiosity is excavation and protection, not generation.
