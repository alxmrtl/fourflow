# Alive
*The Concentrate quality of Ignited Curiosity*

## What it is

When this quality is present, the session sustains itself. The work is interesting enough on its own terms that attention doesn't need to be continuously redirected back to it. Flow is autotelic — the activity rewards itself, making the present engagement the point rather than a means to some future payoff. When absent, the motivation source matters enormously: actions fueled by fear, obligation, or the need to prove something keep the protective ego active — monitoring for failure, managing identity stakes — which fragments the very attention flow requires. Clean fuel (curiosity, genuine interest) quiets the ego because there's nothing to defend. The full attentional field becomes available.

## Science

Self-determination theory (Deci & Ryan, 1985) distinguishes intrinsic motivation (engaging because the activity itself is inherently satisfying) from extrinsic motivation (engaging for separable outcomes). Intrinsic motivation produces greater depth of engagement, higher creativity, and more sustained effort — and crucially, it's self-reinforcing: the reward of engagement increases motivation to re-engage, rather than depleting it.

Dopamine and novelty-seeking neuroscience (Berridge & Kringelbach, 2015) distinguishes the "wanting" system (mesolimbic dopamine, driving anticipation and pursuit) from the "liking" system (opioid circuits, generating satisfaction). Clean intrinsic motivation activates both — genuine curiosity produces both the wanting-pull into the work and the liking-reward within it. Fear-based motivation activates wanting (pursuit) without liking (satisfaction), creating a driven state that never genuinely resolves.

The ego depletion mechanism (Muraven & Baumeister, 2000) explains why dirty fuel fragments attention: effort spent monitoring for failure and managing identity stakes is ego-regulatory effort, which competes directly with the directed attention flow requires. Intrinsic engagement eliminates this competition — there's nothing to monitor for.
