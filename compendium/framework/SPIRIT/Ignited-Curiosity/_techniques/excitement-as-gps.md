# Excitement as GPS

> Treat genuine excitement as directional signal from the soul — not as distraction, indulgence, or noise — and follow it even when the destination isn't visible.

## Mechanism

Excitement isn't random. It's the system signaling that something in this direction is needed — not necessarily what you think is there, but something: a meeting, a realization, a skill, a connection, a transformation. The logical mind evaluates by projected outcome ("how will this help?"). Excitement operates on a different information set — one that can see further than the conscious mind's current vantage point. When you suppress excitement in favor of obligation or "should," you cut off the internal signal most closely aligned with clean fuel. When you follow it — even without knowing where it leads — intrinsic motivation compounds naturally, no willpower required.

The mechanism connects directly to clean/dirty fuel: obligation is dirty fuel (fear-based, ego-active), excitement is clean fuel (curiosity-based, ego-quiet). Following excitement keeps the ego low because there's nothing to prove; the engagement is inherently its own reward.

## Protocol

1. **Check what's most alive** — At any fork (between tasks, at the start of a session, when stuck), ask: "What genuinely excites me most from the options available right now?" Not what's most logical, most urgent, or most expected. What's actually lit up.
2. **Dismiss the logic objection** — The mind will say "but how does this relate to the goal?" Note the objection, don't obey it. The GPS doesn't need to explain the route — it just knows the route.
3. **Move toward it, to the best of your ability** — Take action now, with what you have, as far as you can. Not after more preparation. Not when conditions are right. Now.
4. **Hold zero insistence on where it leads** — The excitement is the compass, not the destination. Where it goes may look nothing like you expected. That's not failure; that's the route.
5. **Repeat** — When you hit a wall or the thread runs out, check in again. What's the next highest excitement from available options? Follow that.

## When to Use

- When motivation feels flat and you're pushing rather than pulled — check for suppressed excitement
- At the start of a work session to find the natural entry point rather than forcing a predetermined one
- When stuck mid-project: "what about this excites me right now?" often reactivates movement
- When multiple options compete: excitement is faster and more accurate than analytical comparison for creative and generative work
- **Not** a replacement for commitment to ongoing work — more like a navigation tool within committed work, not an escape from it

## Key Insight

> "When you feel pulled toward something, that's not distraction. That's DIRECTION. When you feel alive doing something, that's not indulgence. That's ALIGNMENT."
