# Neurochemical Stacking Cascade

> A five-stage progression from curiosity to mastery — each stage building the neurochemical conditions for the next, culminating in self-sustaining intrinsic motivation that doesn't require willpower.

## Protocol

**Stage 1 — Curiosity**: Follow what pulls you without needing to justify it. Don't choose interests rationally; notice what creates forward pull. Biochemistry: dopamine (novelty signal) + norepinephrine (heightened attention). You don't choose your curiosity; you follow it.

**Stage 2 — Passion**: Invest enough to develop the curiosity. Passion isn't found before commitment — it emerges after enough investment to see the territory. Biochemistry: sustained dopamine + norepinephrine. The return on attention begins to compound.

**Stage 3 — Purpose**: Connect the passion to something larger than self. Ask: who benefits? What does this serve beyond you? Actions begin to feel like contributions rather than performances. Biochemistry: additional dopamine + serotonin (significance) + oxytocin (connection to mission).

**Stage 4 — Autonomy**: Protect the ability to direct your own work and choices. Push back against constraints that remove your authorship. Biochemistry: more dopamine + cortisol reduction. The cortisol reduction is significant — autonomy removes the chronic low-level threat signal that degrades creative thinking.

**Stage 5 — Mastery**: Orient toward getting better as the reward itself. Track growth, not just outcomes. Let the process of improvement become intrinsically rewarding. Biochemistry: sustainable dopamine that doesn't require external validation or novel stimuli to maintain.

**The compound effect**: Each stage makes the next more accessible. Skip stages and the cascade doesn't fully build. Stage 5 is where people who are "hard to stop" live — not because of willpower, but because the biochemical reward structure makes continuing more attractive than stopping.

## When to Use
As a design framework when building a new pursuit or project. As a diagnostic when motivation is inconsistent — identify which stage is underdeveloped. As a north star: the question is always "how do I build toward the next stage?"

## Key Insight
> "Most people fall into a rut because they seek extrinsic motivators. Intrinsic motivators at the mastery stage produce dopamine that sustains without escalation — the source of the reward is the activity itself."
