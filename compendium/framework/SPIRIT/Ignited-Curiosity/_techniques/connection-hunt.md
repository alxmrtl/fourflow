# Connection Hunt

> One insight becomes ten when you force it to make contact with a different domain.

## Protocol
1. At the end of any exploration session — a Rabbit Hole Session, a reading block, a learning sprint — stop before closing.
2. Write one sentence summarizing the core insight or shift in understanding from this session.
3. Ask: "What does this remind me of in a completely different domain?" The domain should be genuinely different — not a subspecialty of the same field. Force the reach: literature, biology, music, architecture, sport, history, cooking.
4. Write the connection in one sentence: "This is like [X from domain Y] because [mechanism]." The "because [mechanism]" is mandatory — surface similarity is not a connection; structural similarity is.
5. Log both the insight and the connection in your curiosity ledger or exploration notes.
6. Periodically review your connection log. Recurring structural mechanisms appearing across domains are candidates for deeper investigation — they often point to something fundamental.

## When to Use
- As a closing step at the end of every exploration session — make it non-optional
- When stuck on a problem in one domain — reviewing your connection log from other domains often surfaces a structural analog that unlocks the current problem
- When teaching or explaining something: find the cross-domain connection before presenting — it makes abstract ideas concrete and memorable

## Key Insight
> "The most generative question after any exploration isn't 'what did I learn?' — it's 'what does this rhyme with?'"
