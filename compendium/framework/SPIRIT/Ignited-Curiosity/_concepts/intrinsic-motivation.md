# Intrinsic Motivation

> Deci and Ryan's concept: the drive to act from genuine interest, mastery-seeking, and autonomy — motivation that originates inside the activity itself rather than from external reward or pressure.

## What It Is

Intrinsic motivation, developed through Edward Deci and Richard Ryan's self-determination theory (SDT), describes the motivational quality that arises when an activity is pursued for its inherent interest and enjoyment rather than for separable outcomes (money, grades, approval). The distinction is not about what you do but about why — the perceived locus of causality.

SDT identifies three basic psychological needs whose satisfaction enables intrinsic motivation:
- **Autonomy**: the sense that you are the author of your actions, choosing from genuine values rather than being controlled
- **Competence**: the sense of effectiveness and growing mastery
- **Relatedness**: the sense of meaningful connection to others

When these three are met, intrinsic motivation naturally emerges. When any is undermined — particularly autonomy — extrinsic motivation takes over and intrinsic motivation diminishes. Critically, Deci and Ryan showed that adding external rewards to intrinsically motivated behavior often *decreases* motivation by shifting the perceived locus from internal to external.

## Why It Enables Flow

Intrinsic motivation and flow are deeply aligned: both depend on genuine engagement with the activity for its own sake. Csikszentmihalyi described flow as "autotelic" — the activity is its own reward. Intrinsic motivation is the dispositional precondition for autotelic experience. You cannot reliably access flow in work that is only extrinsically motivated, because the extrinsic frame keeps attention on the outcome rather than the process.

## Application

The diagnostic question: if you were guaranteed no external recognition or reward, would you still find this work worth doing? If yes, intrinsic motivation is present. If no, the honest answer is that you're working for the outcome, not the work — and that's not inherently wrong, but it means flow access will be unreliable. The intervention is either reconnecting to what is genuinely interesting within the work, or restructuring toward work that actually carries intrinsic pull.
