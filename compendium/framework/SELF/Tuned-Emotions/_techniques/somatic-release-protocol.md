# Somatic Release Protocol

> Teach your body — through 50 repetitions of physically dropping an object — what releasing actually feels like, so you can access that state on demand.

## Protocol

1. Gather a variety of objects that can be safely dropped onto a soft surface (carpet, towel, cushion): pens, books, pillows, clothing items, anything within reach.
2. Pick up the first object. Hold it deliberately — feel the weight.
3. Grip it slightly tighter, noticing the tension in your hand and forearm.
4. Drop it. Pay close attention to the **exact moment of release** — the sensation of tension leaving your hand, the slight opening of the fingers, the brief sense of lightness.
5. Pause for one breath. Notice what letting go felt like in your body.
6. Move to the next object. Repeat.
7. Complete at least 50 drops in one session. Vary objects, vary grip intensity, vary timing — but maintain the attention on the release sensation.

**Over the following days**: when you notice emotional tension, resistance, or a grip you can't seem to loosen — invoke the drop. Not the concept of dropping, but the body-felt memory from the session: that specific sensation of hand opening and tension releasing. Let the emotional state follow the somatic cue.

## When to Use

- As a one-time installation practice (one dedicated session of 50 drops), then periodically refreshed (monthly)
- Before high-stakes situations where you know you'll need to release outcomes or pressure
- When pre-session emotional clearing (see [[pre-session-clearance]]) isn't landing — something more somatic is needed
- When verbal or cognitive releasing practices feel hollow or don't produce state change

## Key Insight

> "You already know how to let go — you've done it thousands of times. This practice builds a shortcut from body memory to emotional state."
