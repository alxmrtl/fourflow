# Resistance-Desire Clearing

> Neutralize the emotional charge on your strongest aversions and desires — not by eliminating them, but by reducing their grip from 9/10 to 6/10 — to reclaim the attentional bandwidth they consume.

## Protocol

**Part 1 — Build Your Lists (15 minutes)**
1. Write a Resistance List: situations, people, qualities, sensations, or outcomes you actively avoid, dislike, or fear. Be honest and specific. ("Being ignored." "My brother-in-law." "Silence at dinner." "Losing the project.")
2. Write a Desire List: things you crave, long for, feel pulled toward compulsively, or can't stop thinking about wanting. Be honest and specific. ("Approval from my team." "The deal closing." "Her attention." "More time.")

**Part 2 — Neutralize Resistances**
3. Take one item from your Resistance List. Rate its current charge: 0–10.
4. Deliberately imagine *wanting* this thing. Create genuine desire toward it — not forced positivity, but a real search for why someone might want it, and feeling that. Hold the desire feeling.
5. Continue until you feel the intensity of the original resistance begin to drop. Stop when it reaches 6–7/10. Do not try to reach zero.
6. Move to the next resistance item.

**Part 3 — Neutralize Desires**
7. Take one item from your Desire List. Rate its current charge: 0–10.
8. Deliberately imagine *resisting* this thing — creating genuine aversion or indifference toward it. Hold the resistant feeling.
9. Continue until the intensity of the original desire drops to 6–7/10. Stop there.

**Notes**:
- Work one session per week: 3–5 items per list per session
- Items with the highest charge are the highest value targets
- The 5–6/10 neutral zone is the goal — not detachment, not suppression, just reduced pull

## When to Use

Before major work periods when you know background emotional loads are running — unresolved conflicts, strong desires for specific outcomes, fears about particular results. Also useful as a periodic life hygiene practice (monthly) to catch charges that accumulate silently. Not a crisis intervention tool — use [[pre-session-clearance]] for same-day emotional weather.

## Key Insight

> "You don't need to stop wanting things or stop fearing things. You just need those wants and fears to stop running in the background while you're trying to work."
