# Error Tolerance

> Allows rapid correction without identity threat.

## Mechanism

Flow requires full engagement with the task at hand. When errors carry identity threat — when each mistake is evidence of inadequacy rather than information about the path — a fraction of attention is always monitoring for failure. This surveillance is incompatible with absorption. Error tolerance keeps the feedback loop between mistake and correction running cleanly, without the detour through self-evaluation that slows correction and fragments attention. At scale, across a project or a life, error tolerance is the variable that determines how many iterations a person can run — and therefore how capable they ultimately become.

## Techniques

- [[portfolio-of-failures-reframe]] — treats each mistake as a deposit into the experience portfolio rather than evidence of inadequacy; extracts the information and continues
- [[self-driving-car-iteration]] — frames each attempt as one data point in an iteration sequence; separates iteration from identity entirely
- [[uncertainty-as-signal]] — reframes feeling lost or overwhelmed as confirmation of being in the right territory (learning zone), not a warning to retreat
- [[load-bearing-behaviour-reframe]] — reframes persistent "failures" as load-bearing structures: you are not fighting a bad habit, you are replacing a load-bearing function; never remove without replacing

## Recall

**Hook:** People are so afraid of making mistakes that they make the biggest mistake of all — not making mistakes.
**Mechanism:** Errors carry identity threat only when outcomes are tied to self-worth; decoupling the two keeps the feedback loop between mistake and correction running cleanly, without the self-evaluation detour that slows everything down.
**Anchor:** Portfolio Reframe — when a mistake occurs, ask "What does this add to my portfolio?" not "What does this say about me?" Extract the information, adjust, continue.
