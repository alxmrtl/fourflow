# Recognizing Heart State

> A real-time check for whether the heart is open or closed — used several times daily to catch pattern activation early and convert it into Re-Patterning material.

## Mechanism
The presence or absence of open heart qualities is a real-time diagnostic of internal state. The act of noticing itself begins to shift the relationship to the pattern — you become an observer of the state rather than being identified with it. Unnoticed closed-heart states run the session; noticed ones become material for transformation. Earlier detection = smaller intervention needed.

## Protocol

1. Several times daily — at session start, after breaks, after any interaction — pause and ask: "Is my heart open or closed right now?"
2. Scan for signs:
   - **Open signs**: ease, relaxation, warmth, trust, presence, willingness to engage, enjoyment of process regardless of outcome, creative flow
   - **Closed signs**: anxiety, defensiveness, judgment of self or others, need for certainty or control, self-consciousness, performing rather than being, well-being contingent on results
3. Don't judge what you find — just notice. "The heart is a bit closed right now" is neutral information, not failure.
4. If closed: ask "What pattern is causing this?" Note it for Re-Patterning later. Optionally, use [[open-heart-state]] practice to restore access in the moment.
5. If open: acknowledge it and begin or continue work.

**Conditional vs. unconditional well-being check**: When the heart is closed, it often reveals conditional well-being patterns ("I'll feel safe when this project succeeds," "I can relax after I get approval," "I'll be OK once I figure this out"). These create a perpetual chase — safety is never stable because conditions are never permanently met. Noticing the condition reveals the pattern: what is your well-being contingent on right now?

## When to Use
Several times daily as a background practice. Most important: before sessions (to start from the right orientation), after challenging interactions (to catch post-social pattern activation), and mid-session when something shifts (output quality drops, avoidance appears, self-consciousness spikes).
