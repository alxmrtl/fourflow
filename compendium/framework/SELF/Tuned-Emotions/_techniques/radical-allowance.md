# Radical Allowance

> Release your grip on HOW the outcome arrives — keep full clarity on what you want and the felt state of having it, then step out of the logistics entirely.

## Mechanism

Most people fail at non-attachment not because they can't let go of the outcome, but because they've collapsed the outcome with a specific delivery pathway. They want the thing AND insist it arrive via the route they identified. When that route stalls, they experience it as the outcome not coming — and exhaust themselves forcing it. Radical Allowance separates the want (valid, yours) from the how (not yours). The moment you release the how, every available pathway opens — including ones you couldn't perceive from your current vantage point. The outcome arrives through an unexpected direction precisely because you stopped blocking the unexpected directions. This isn't passivity: you still take action, specifically the action dictated by your excitement rather than by your logical route-plan.

## Protocol

**The Efforting Pattern** (what to replace):
1. Want something
2. Identify HOW you think it should come
3. Push and strain toward that specific how
4. Block all other pathways — fixated on the chosen route
5. Exhaust yourself; receive nothing
6. Conclude it doesn't work

**The Radical Allowance Protocol**:
1. **State the want clearly** — be specific about what you actually want, without hedging
2. **Feel the having** — spend 30–60 seconds inhabiting the state of already having it. Not visualization for its own sake — the felt quality of the receiving state
3. **Release the how entirely** — explicitly name and then drop the delivery mechanism you've been attached to: "The how is not my job"
4. **Follow excitement** — take whatever action feels most alive right now, even if it seems unrelated to the outcome
5. **Allow delivery from any direction** — when movement comes toward your want, from any unexpected source, receive it without insisting it look different
6. **Repeat** — the want is a standing instruction; the allowance is a daily practice of not obstructing delivery

## When to Use

- When you notice yourself straining, forcing, or "trying too hard" toward a specific outcome
- When the logical path is blocked and you're tempted to keep hammering the same door
- When you've been working on something for a long time with diminishing returns — the attachment to the how may be the block
- When anxiety about an outcome is rising — Radical Allowance is the structural practice; anxiety-suppression handles the acute signal
- **Not** a justification for inaction — the protocol still requires following excitement and taking action; it only releases the specific how, not effort itself

## Key Insight

> "The want is your job. The having is your job. The how is NOT your job. The how is the universe's job — and it has access to variables you can't even perceive."
