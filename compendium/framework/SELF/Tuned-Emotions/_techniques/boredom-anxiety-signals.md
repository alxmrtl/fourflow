# Boredom and Anxiety as Navigation Signals

> Treat boredom and anxiety as calibration feedback, not as malfunctions — each signals a specific relationship between your current challenge and your current capability.

## Protocol

1. When boredom appears during non-focus time (not mid-session), ask: "What would genuinely challenge my current capability?" Use the boredom as a signal to seek harder problems, new domains, or creative projects that match actual capacity.
2. When anxiety appears outside of a focus session, ask: "Am I approaching my capability edge?" Distinguish pattern-driven anxiety (familiar emotional reaction to a class of situations) from calibration-driven anxiety (genuine skill gap). Pattern-driven → use [[anxiety-suppression]] protocols. Calibration-driven → use [[sub-goal-decomposition]] or skill-building.
3. During focus sessions, both signals call for calibration adjustment — not resistance or endurance. Boredom → raise the challenge. Anxiety → decompose.

## When to Use
As a general interpretive frame applied throughout the day, not just during work. Most useful during transitions between tasks, project selection, and career/creative direction decisions where these signals are rich information about where you are relative to your capability frontier.

**Important distinction**: Inside a focus session, boredom and anxiety are immediate calibration signals to adjust now. Outside a focus session, they're navigation signals for broader direction.

## Key Insight
> "Boredom is a gateway to novelty. Anxiety is the edge of capability. Neither is the enemy — misreading them is."
