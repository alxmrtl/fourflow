# Under-Load Entry Protocol

> A 5-second triage sequence for moments when pressure has risen and clarity has collapsed — two questions that locate exactly what's happening and where.

## Mechanism
Under load, the decision system terminates questions at the first level that can carry the load. What begins as a higher-order question (about principle, identity, commitment) gets reduced to whatever ends the tension fastest. The descent happens automatically and unconsciously — you don't decide to lower your standards, you just find yourself acting from a lower level. This micro-protocol interrupts the automatic descent by forcing two precise questions before the system settles. It doesn't require calm, analysis, or self-knowledge — just enough metacognitive capacity to ask two questions. It compresses the full diagnostic into something executable when you're already under pressure.

## Protocol

1. **Notice the pressure signal.** Any of: reaching for relief, feeling urgency to "just do something," awareness that you're not thinking clearly, impulse toward something you know you'll regret.
2. **Ask Question 1: "What problem is being solved?"** Not "what should I be doing?" — what is this behaviour *actually solving* right now? Name the function: tension reduction, contradiction resolution, short-term coherence, relief from uncertainty.
3. **Ask Question 2: "Where has the question stopped?"** At which level is the system currently operating?
   - Impulse/relief seeking (L0 — behaviour)
   - "This feels right" (L1 — authority)
   - "The rules allow it" (L2 — governance)
   - "This is who I am" (L3 — identity/covenant)
   - Something non-negotiable (L4 — worship/values)
4. **Name the gap.** The gap between where the question stopped and where it should be answered is the mismatch. Most problems are handled one or two layers too low.
5. **Choose one move:** Either reduce the load (remove a stressor) or accept the current level without fighting it ("not today" rather than "never again"). Do not force upward.

## When to Use
The moment you feel the pull toward relief-seeking behaviour under pressure. Before reaching for the phone, the snack, the argument-ending concession, the "just this once" override. Also useful as a post-hoc analysis tool: after acting under pressure, run the two questions to understand what happened without self-judgment. Builds pattern literacy over time.

**Limit**: Requires minimal self-observation capacity. Under extreme physiological stress, coercion, or neurological override, the system cannot run even this protocol. In those conditions, stabilise the body first (L0).

## Key Insight
> "Under load, do not analyse everything. First: what problem is being solved? Second: where has the question stopped?"
