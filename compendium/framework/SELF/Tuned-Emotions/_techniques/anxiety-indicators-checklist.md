# Anxiety Indicators Checklist

> A diagnostic scan of closed-heart reactions — any match signals pattern activation and becomes material for re-patterning.

## Mechanism
The absence of open heart qualities reveals pattern activation. These reactions aren't character flaws or problems to eliminate directly — they're indicators that a pattern is running. Treating them as diagnostic signals (rather than things to fight) shifts the relationship from struggle to observation, which is the precondition for transformation.

## Protocol

1. When focus breaks, or when something feels off, scan this list:
   - Anxiety, nervousness, second-guessing
   - Seeking maximal certainty, needing reassurance
   - Needing control, trying to figure it out
   - Procrastination, unwarranted cautiousness
   - Being in a hurry, feeling time pressure
   - Perfectionism, needing to do it right
   - Neuroticism about any life area
   - Avoiding and hiding, scarcity thinking
   - Self-doubt, unfounded meaning-making

2. Any match = loggable disturbance. Note it in the Disturbance Log.
3. Do not try to eliminate the reaction in the moment. The checklist's job is identification, not correction.

## When to Use
As a daily diagnostic — particularly at the start of work sessions, after difficult interactions, or whenever something feels subtly off but you can't name it. The list externalizes pattern activation that would otherwise remain vague discomfort. It converts "I feel off" into specific material.

## Key Insight
> "The reaction isn't the problem — it's an invitation to see and transform what's underneath."
