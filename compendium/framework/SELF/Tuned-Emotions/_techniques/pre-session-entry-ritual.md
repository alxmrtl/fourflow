# Pre-Session Entry Ritual

> A consistent, repeatable 3–10 minute sequence that transitions the nervous system from evaluative mode into executive/process mode — conditioning the hypofrontal state over time.

## Mechanism
Pre-performance routines work because they transition the nervous system from evaluative mode (Am I ready? What if I fail?) to executive mode (I do this now). The ritual has to be consistent enough to become a conditioned state-shift — over time, the ritual itself becomes the trigger for the transient hypofrontal state that enables flow. Every repeated ritual that has preceded productive sessions becomes a conditioned cue for the neural state those sessions required. Athletes with no ritual have no clear transition point for self-consciousness to shut off; their entry to performance mode is random.

## Protocol

1. **Designate the ritual** — a specific, repeatable sequence of 3–10 minutes containing three elements:
   - (a) **Physical element**: breathwork, a specific stretch, a brief walk, or a physical transition (sit in the chair, put on headphones).
   - (b) **Mental element**: one sentence about what you're here to do, not what result you want. ("I'm here to move the argument in section 2 forward" — not "I need to write something great.")
   - (c) **Start signal**: a single clear action that marks the boundary — turning on a specific lamp, opening a specific app, pressing play on a specific playlist. This signal is the trigger.

2. **Keep it identical** — variation reduces its conditioning power. Same sequence, same order, every session.

3. **Do not evaluate mood before starting** — the ritual does not require feeling ready. It requires performing the ritual. The state follows the action.

4. **Begin work immediately upon the start signal** — no pause for preparation or mood assessment after the signal fires.

## When to Use
Before every focused work session. The conditioning power compounds with repetition — a ritual used for 30 consecutive sessions will produce a stronger state-shift than one used sporadically. Establish the ritual during a low-stakes period so it is already conditioned before high-stakes sessions.

## Key Insight
> "You cannot decide to stop monitoring yourself. You can decide to start the ritual. The monitoring stops on its own."
