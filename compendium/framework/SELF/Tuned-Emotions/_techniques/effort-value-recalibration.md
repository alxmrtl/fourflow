# Effort-Value Recalibration

> Front-load an external reward contingent on completing focused work — used during skill acquisition or project initiations when natural reward is too distant to sustain motivation.

## Mechanism
Effort can acquire intrinsic value when consistently paired with reward — but only if the reward is salient enough and sufficiently contingent on the effort (Capa et al., 2022, *PNAS*). Early-stage effort on any skill or project is high-cost, low-reward. If you rely on the natural reward to materialize, motivation often doesn't survive the early phase. Effort-value recalibration front-loads reward to bridge the gap — and over 4–6 weeks, the intrinsic value transfers: the effort itself begins to feel rewarding even without the external signal. This is conditioning, not willpower.

## Protocol

1. Identify a reward you genuinely value and can deliver to yourself *contingent on completing a session* (not on output quality — on showing up and working). Examples: the specific coffee you love, 20 minutes of a show, a walk in nature, a social call.
2. Make the contingency explicit before the session: "When I complete 90 minutes of focused work on this, I get X."
3. Complete the session.
4. Deliver the reward **immediately** upon completion — not after other tasks, not later in the day. The coupling only works if the reward is temporally adjacent to the effort.
5. Maintain this structure for 4–6 weeks. After this period, assess whether the effort itself has begun to feel rewarding independent of the external signal.

## When to Use
During skill acquisition phases, project initiations, or any period when the natural reward signal is too distant to sustain motivation (early stage of a book, learning a new language, starting a fitness practice). Also useful for breaking procrastination on important but inherently unrewarding tasks.

**Contraindication**: Do not use this technique as a permanent structure for work you should intrinsically value. It is a bridge, not a destination. If external rewards remain necessary after 6–8 weeks, that may signal a deeper calibration or values-alignment issue.

## Key Insight
> "You can learn to find effort rewarding. The mechanism is conditioning — not willpower. Front-load the reward while the brain is building the association."
