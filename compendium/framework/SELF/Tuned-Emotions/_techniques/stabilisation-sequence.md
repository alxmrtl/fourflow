# Stabilisation Sequence

> A layered rebuild protocol for restoring alignment after collapse — stability first, clarity second, enforcement last. You cannot skip layers under load.

## Mechanism
When the system has collapsed under pressure, the instinctive response is to go high — reassert principles, recommit to identity, invoke values. But if lower layers are unstable, this concentrates instability rather than resolving it. Attempting to enforce higher-order standards while the base is shaking doesn't produce coherence — it removes the only load-shedding mechanisms the system has, and the accumulated instability eventually produces catastrophic failure rather than the distributed, manageable collapse it replaced. The sequence matters: stability precedes clarity, grounding precedes enforcement. Each layer must hold before the next can bear weight.

## Protocol

1. **Stabilise behaviour (L0).** Stop the bleeding. What is happening right now? Name it without judgment. If the system is in active load-shedding (numbing, avoiding, compulsively acting), don't try to stop it — redirect it. Find a less destructive version of the same function. Reduce immediate load: remove triggers, simplify environment, reduce inputs.
2. **Re-establish what feels right (L1).** Once behaviour is stable, reconnect to felt sense. Not rules, not principles — what feels genuinely right in the body and heart? The open-heart state check lives here. If the heart is closed, work with that before going higher.
3. **Reinforce governance (L2).** Once felt sense is restored, re-establish the rules and structures that support the system. Not new rules — the ones that were already working before collapse. Minimal structure that holds without strain.
4. **Rebuild commitments (L3).** Only when L0–L2 are stable: reconnect to identity-level commitments. Who am I in this? What am I committed to? These commitments must be ones the system can actually sustain at its current capacity — not aspirational versions.
5. **Reconnect to what is non-negotiable (L4).** The deepest layer. What is not subject to revision? Only re-anchor here when the entire structure below can hold the weight. A forced L4 reconnection with unstable lower layers is the most dangerous intervention in the sequence.

**Critical failure mode**: Do not trade distributed collapse for ruinous collapse by forcing L4 anchoring while L0–L2 are unstable. The instability doesn't disappear — it accumulates.

## When to Use
After any significant collapse in alignment: a period of burnout, a relapse, a betrayal of your own standards, an emotional breakdown, a loss of direction. Also preventively: when you notice the system beginning to descend (principle becoming rule becoming justification becoming impulse), stabilise at the current level rather than trying to force ascent.

**Order is non-negotiable under load.** In calm conditions, you can work multiple layers simultaneously. Under load, the sequence must be respected.

> "Stability precedes clarity. Grounding precedes enforcement. You cannot skip layers under load."
