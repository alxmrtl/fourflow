# Thought Stopping + Redirect

> A two-step interruption for when the evaluative voice breaks through mid-session: Stop (interrupt the loop before it builds), then Redirect immediately to a pre-established process cue.

## Mechanism
Self-evaluation loops sustain themselves through attention. The thought generates an emotional response, the emotional response generates another thought, and the loop grows. Interruption at step one — before the emotional response escalates — breaks the loop before it becomes self-sustaining. The redirect must be immediate and pre-designated: having a process cue already established means the redirect is automatic, not deliberated. Deliberating gives the evaluative loop time to reload.

## Protocol

1. When the evaluative voice activates mid-session ("this is terrible," "I'm not good at this," "what are they going to think"):
2. **Stop**: deliver a brief, firm internal command — "Stop." or "Not now." Optionally use a physical cue to ground the interruption in the body: snap a wristband, press thumb and forefinger together, tap a desk. The physicality makes it harder for the loop to re-engage immediately.
3. **Redirect**: immediately move attention to the process cue established before the session. Do not address the thought's content. Do not argue with it. Do not assess whether it's accurate. Redirect to process.
4. Return to work. If the evaluative voice re-activates within the same minute, repeat the two-step without escalating.

## When to Use
Any moment the inner critic activates during a performance session. Most effective when the redirect target (the process cue) has been established before the session. If no process cue was set up, the redirect is "what is the next specific action?" — narrow the focus to the smallest possible next step.

**Note**: This technique manages active evaluative loops; it doesn't prevent them. Process Focus, External Focus, and Pre-Session Entry Ritual work upstream to reduce the frequency of loops in the first place. Use Thought Stopping + Redirect when the upstream techniques didn't fully prevent activation.

## Key Insight
> "You don't have to defeat the inner critic. You have to redirect before it builds momentum."
