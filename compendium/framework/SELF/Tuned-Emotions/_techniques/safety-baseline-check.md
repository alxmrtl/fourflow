# Safety Baseline Check

> A 60-second somatic scan before high-stakes sessions — detects whether threat circuitry is active, names the threat to reduce it, and reconnects to the heart before work begins.

## Mechanism
The body announces fear before the mind does. When the amygdala fires, it produces physical contraction (tight chest, elevated shoulders, clenched jaw) before the conscious narrative of fear has formed. Detecting the contraction early allows naming the threat (which activates the prefrontal cortex and immediately begins reducing amygdala firing — Lieberman et al., 2007) before the threat response has fully captured attentional resources. The final step — directing attention to the heart — initiates the open heart reconnection that restores the calm aperture flow requires.

## Protocol

1. **Scan for physical contraction**: Is the chest tight? Shoulders elevated? Jaw clenched? These are the body's threat signatures — amygdala has fired, the body is in protective mode.
2. **Name the threat** if contraction is present: complete the sentence "I'm afraid this will be judged as..." or "I'm worried that..." Naming the threat activates the prefrontal cortex and immediately begins reducing amygdala firing.
3. **Reality-test the threat**: "What's the worst plausible outcome here, and could I survive it?" The answer is almost always yes. This is not toxic positivity — it's genuine risk assessment that the fear response routinely inflates.
4. **Shift to the heart**: One breath directed at the center of the chest, with the intention of warmth. This is the beginning of open heart reconnection. No belief required — just directed attention and breath.
5. Begin work immediately after step 4.

## When to Use
Before creative sessions, performance situations, or any context where self-consciousness is likely. Particularly useful before: sharing creative work for the first time, presenting to a new audience, collaborating with someone whose judgment you fear, starting a session after a difficult interaction or criticism.

The check takes 60 seconds. The return is reduced mid-session threat response and wider attentional aperture from the start.

## Key Insight
> "The body announces fear before the mind does. The safety check reads the announcement and responds before it takes over."
