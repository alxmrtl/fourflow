# Flow Cycle Phases

> Every flow session moves through four phases: Struggle → Release → Flow → Recovery. Aborting at Struggle is the most common error.

## The Four Phases

**1. Struggle**
The prefrontal cortex is resisting. Attention keeps slipping. It feels like the task isn't working, or like you're inadequate for it. This is not a signal to stop — it's the entry tax. Duration: roughly the first 15–25% of a session. The brain is loading, not failing.

**2. Release**
Resistance dissolves. Not with a bang — usually a quiet shift. The task starts pulling rather than pushing. This is the transition window. It's fragile: interruptions here reset the cycle back to Struggle.

**3. Flow**
Transient hypofrontality — the prefrontal cortex (the self-monitoring, self-judging part) goes partially offline. The inner critic quiets. Time distorts. The work runs itself. Neurochemically: dopamine, norepinephrine, anandamide, serotonin, endorphins — all elevated simultaneously.

**4. Recovery**
Flow is metabolically expensive. The brain needs to consolidate and restore. Skipping recovery shortens the next flow session. Chronic recovery-skipping degrades the system's ability to enter flow at all.

## Why It Matters for Tuned Emotions

The most common Tuned Emotions failure happens in Struggle: the discomfort is misread as evidence that something is wrong (challenge too high, skills too low, wrong task) and the session is abandoned. In reality, Struggle is the cost of entry — neurobiologically necessary because the brain is transitioning from beta-wave task-switching mode to alpha/theta flow state.

Knowing the cycle converts the Struggle experience from threat signal to confirmation signal: *"I'm in Struggle — this means I'm on track."*

## Application

- **Don't abort at Struggle** — the 25% threshold rule: if you've been in it for less than 25% of your intended session time, you haven't earned the right to stop yet
- **Protect the Release window** — no notifications, no context switches; this is where the session either opens or resets
- **Schedule Recovery** — treat it as part of the session, not wasted time; light walks, non-demanding activities, sleep
