# Challenge-Skill Calibration

> Matches task difficulty to current capability to prevent boredom or overwhelm.

## Why It Enables Flow

Flow requires a specific ratio between what the task demands and what the person can currently deliver. Too much challenge and the nervous system shifts into threat mode — anxiety floods in, self-consciousness spikes, attention turns inward. Too little challenge and the mind wanders toward what it finds more interesting — boredom triggers novelty-seeking and focus fragments. The zone between these two failure modes is narrow but reliable: when challenge is just barely above current skill, the brain has enough to engage fully without triggering threat responses. This is the structural condition for flow, independent of motivation or discipline — fix the ratio and focus follows.
