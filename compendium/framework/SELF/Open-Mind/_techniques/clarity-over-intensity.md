# Clarity Over Intensity

When stuck, substitute "work harder" with "understand more clearly" — force narrows the aperture, precision opens it.

## Protocol

1. When effort stops producing progress, pause. Do not increase intensity.
2. Ask: "What exactly is the obstacle?" Not "How can I push through this?" — specificity about the problem is the lever, not force.
3. Restate the problem in writing in the most precise terms you can. Often the restatement reveals the actual block.
4. If emotional urgency is present, name it: "I'm feeling [rushed / anxious / desperate]." Naming it partially dissolves it, restoring the wider aperture.
5. Return to work from a calm, inquisitive stance — "what happens if I try this?" — not the pressured stance.

*If intensity keeps returning, check whether the session goal is too high-stakes or the timeline too compressed. Structural pressure generates emotional intensity.*

## When to Use

- When stuck on a problem and the instinct is to push harder
- When urgency or deadline pressure is visibly narrowing your thinking
- When you notice yourself repeating the same approach with more force rather than trying a different one

## Recall

**Hook:** When stuck, substitute "try harder" with "understand more clearly." Force narrows the aperture; clarity opens it.
**Mechanism:** High emotional intensity constricts the perceptual aperture — the more desperately you want the result, the less clearly you can see the path to it. Calm understanding accesses the wide aperture where insight and pattern recognition operate.
**Anchor:** When effort stops producing progress, pause and ask: "What exactly is the obstacle?" not "How can I work harder?" Precision about the problem is the lever.
