# Pattern Literacy

> Develop the capacity to recognize which of the fundamental unhealthy patterns is activating — so it can be named and brought to Re-Patterning rather than running unexamined.

## Mechanism
You cannot transform a pattern you haven't recognized. Growth orientation requires pattern literacy: the ability to identify which fixed ways of responding to difficulty are activating, and to treat them as material rather than as truth. Each recognized pattern becomes a Re-Patterning opportunity; unrecognized patterns run the show unconsciously. The 10 fundamental patterns are a recognition vocabulary — learning them makes the invisible architecture of reactivity visible.

## The 10 Fundamental Patterns (Source: Conscious Accomplishment)

1. **Polarized Thinking** — viewing things in absolutes (black/white, good/bad, right/wrong)
2. **Over-generalization** — jumping to conclusions; one instance becomes "always" or "never"
3. **Unfounded Suspicion** — over-emphasizing negative possibilities without evidence
4. **Negativity/Pessimism** — automatically assuming the worst
5. **Fixed Expectations** — believing things should be a certain way matching your preferences
6. **Greediness** — striving for more than actually needed; goalpost always moves
7. **Self-centeredness** — over-prioritizing self in ways that damage relationships and blind to others
8. **Avoiding and Hiding** — orchestrating life to avoid facing confrontation, truth, challenge
9. **Stubbornness** — clinging to position regardless of new information; identity attached to being right
10. **Competitiveness/Fight for Dominance** — viewing situations as zero-sum; winning requires others losing

**Additional patterns** (extended library): approval seeking, arrogance, blame and victimhood, clinging to change, comparison, criticism, defensiveness, desire to be special, entitlement, excessive restraint, feeling not enough time, holding extreme expectations, indecisiveness, jealousy, judgment, lack of trust, manipulation, needing certainty, needing to know, obligation, over-accommodation, perfectionism, pride, righteousness/need to be right, scarcity, self-abandonment, self-doubt, self-judgment, skepticism, trying to control, trying to figure it out.

## Protocol

1. **Learn the vocabulary**: Read the 10 fundamental patterns until you can recall them without reference.
2. **Choose 1–2 to focus on initially**: Which show up most frequently in your disturbance log or daily life? Start with those.
3. **Practice recognition in real time**: When a disturbance arises, pause and ask: "Which of the patterns is this?" Just naming it shifts the relationship from identification to observation.
4. **Bring recognized patterns to Re-Patterning** (see [[consciousness-re-patterning]]): Don't try to change patterns through willpower — recognition is the first step; transformation happens in the Re-Patterning session.
5. **Expand gradually**: Once the first 1–2 patterns are reliably recognized, expand awareness to others. Pattern literacy deepens along two dimensions: scope (types of triggers you can recognize) and subtlety (how early you catch the activation before it escalates).

**Paradox note**: As awareness expands, life can feel more disturbing at first — you're seeing patterns that were always there but invisible. This isn't regression; it's progress. You now have material that was previously running unconsciously.

## When to Use
As an ongoing daily practice embedded in the Disturbance Log process. Any time a disturbance arises, apply pattern recognition before recording. Over time, the recognition gap (time between activation and awareness) shortens from hours to minutes to seconds.

## Key Insight
> "Every challenge that triggers you is revealing a pattern — and visible patterns can be transformed."
