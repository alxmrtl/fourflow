# Termination Level Awareness

> Recognises when cognitive overload is causing the system to settle for answers at a lower level than the question requires — premature termination of inquiry driven by working memory exhaustion.

## Mechanism
Under cognitive load, the decision system terminates questions at the first level that can carry the load. A question asked at the level of principle gets answered at the level of convenience. A question asked at the level of identity gets answered at the level of impulse. This isn't laziness — it's the system doing triage with insufficient resources. Working memory is finite; when it's overloaded (from open loops, environmental noise, emotional residue, decision fatigue), the prefrontal cortex cannot sustain the processing depth required for higher-order questions. The system doesn't fail — it settles. The descent pattern is predictable: principle → rule → justification → impulse. Each step trades depth for speed. Recognising where termination is occurring allows you to either reduce load (so the system can sustain the higher level) or consciously defer the decision until capacity is restored.

## Protocol

1. **Catch the settlement.** Notice when you're about to resolve something important with: "just pick one," "it doesn't matter," "whatever works," "I'll figure it out later." These are termination signals — the system is settling below the level the question deserves.
2. **Identify the question's true level.** What level does this question actually need to be answered at?
   - Practical/logistical → low-level resolution is fine
   - Strategic/directional → needs sustained processing, not snap judgment
   - Identity/values → needs the deepest capacity; settling here creates long-term misalignment
3. **Check current cognitive load.** How loaded is the system? (Many open loops, decision fatigue, emotional residue, environmental chaos.) High load = the system will terminate early. This is not optional.
4. **Choose:**
   - **Reduce load, then re-engage.** Close tabs, write down open loops, simplify the environment, eat. Return to the question when working memory has cleared.
   - **Consciously defer.** If load can't be reduced now, explicitly shelve the question: "This needs more capacity than I have right now. I will answer this [when]." Deferral is not avoidance — it's load management.
   - **Accept the current level.** For genuinely low-stakes questions, settling is fine. The technique is about catching premature termination on questions that matter.
5. **Never make identity-level decisions under cognitive overload.** The decision will be made at the wrong level and create misalignment that compounds.

## When to Use
When making decisions while cognitively loaded (end of day, mid-crisis, after sustained focus, during periods of high open-loop count). When you notice the quality of your thinking has degraded but the questions haven't gotten simpler. When you feel the urge to "just decide and move on" about something that deserves more depth.

## Key Insight
> "Under load, the system doesn't fail — it settles. Principle becomes rule becomes justification becomes impulse. The descent is automatic; awareness of it is not."
