# No Mind Practice

> Suspend the self that watches, judges, and evaluates — until only pure awareness remains, without a narrator.

## Protocol

1. Sit still in an upright position. Close your eyes or rest gaze softly on a neutral point.
2. Give yourself this instruction: **"Nobody is home. I am not here."** Let that land.
3. Release the internal voice that judges ("am I doing this right?"), evaluates ("this is working / not working"), calculates ("how much longer?"), or monitors ("I wonder if I look weird right now"). One by one, let each function go quiet — not by suppressing, but by simply not engaging it.
4. Rest in pure awareness: no self watching, no commentary running, no position to defend. Just the experience of existing, unobserved.
5. Hold this for a minimum of 5 minutes. Longer is better. When the self-watcher re-asserts, gently release it again without making an event of the return.
6. After the practice: identify **one specific situation today** where you'll attempt to transfer this state — a meeting, a work session, a conversation. Note what the self-watcher usually does in that context, and practice not feeding it.

**What to expect**: the first attempts feel strange or like "I'm not doing anything." That's correct. The monitoring ego expects a task; the absence of task is what you're practicing.

## When to Use

- As a standalone practice before high-stakes situations where self-consciousness is a known blocker (presentations, creative output, athletic performance, important conversations)
- As a mid-session reset when you catch yourself in a monitoring loop during work
- As a daily 5–10 minute sit to build familiarity with the selfless awareness state

Not a replacement for [[pre-session-clearance]] — that handles emotional clearing. This handles the structural observer layer.

## Key Insight

> "The monitoring ego requires active maintenance. Stop feeding it — not by fighting it, but by simply not being home — and the merging is what's left."
