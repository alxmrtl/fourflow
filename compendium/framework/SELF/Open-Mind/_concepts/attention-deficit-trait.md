# Attention Deficit Trait

> An acquired cognitive fragmentation syndrome — the brain operating in permanent low-grade crisis mode as a response to chronic information overload. Not ADD. No genetic component. Reversible.

## What It Is

Attention Deficit Trait (ADT) was named by psychiatrist Edward Hallowell to distinguish an environmentally-caused syndrome from the genetic condition ADHD. ADT develops in response to chronic overload: too many demands, too many inputs, too many open loops, sustained over time.

The brain's response is to activate survival-mode processing: prioritizing novelty and potential threats over sustained analytical work. The mind loses resolution — it becomes harder to plan, set priorities, think deeply, or experience satisfaction. The person feels perpetually busy but unproductive, always behind, never able to catch up.

The distinguishing feature from ADD: ADT has no genetic component and is fully reversible. The brain developed it as a learned response and can unlearn it given sufficient deload.

## Why It Blocks Flow

ADT is perhaps the single most comprehensive blocker of flow in modern life. Flow requires the brain to shift from task-monitoring to full absorption. ADT keeps the brain in monitoring mode by design — it has learned that the environment demands constant vigilance. Flow becomes structurally inaccessible, not from lack of ability or motivation, but because the biological conditions for absorption have been trained out.

## Application

ADT is a whole-system condition requiring a whole-system intervention. The highest-leverage entry point is cognitive load reduction: externalizing every open loop onto paper or a trusted system so the brain holds nothing in working memory beyond the present task. Followed by sustained periods of single-task focus, even if brief — training the brain that deep absorption is safe again.
