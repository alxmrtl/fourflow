# Dopamine Baseline

> The sensitivity threshold that determines what registers as rewarding. Chronic overstimulation raises this baseline until ordinary deep work feels boring and flow's struggle phase becomes unbearable.

## What It Is

Dopamine is not simply the "pleasure chemical" — it is primarily the seeking and anticipation signal. It fires in response to novelty, reward cues, and information that might lead to reward. The brain calibrates this system dynamically: repeated high-stimulation inputs raise the baseline threshold, making ordinary stimuli feel flat.

When the baseline is elevated — from chronic social media use, constant notifications, or information overload — the brain requires increasingly intense inputs to register engagement. The slow, uncertain, gradually-deepening experience of deep work no longer provides enough stimulation signal to feel worthwhile. It registers as boredom even when it's the work you most want to do.

## Why It Blocks Flow

Flow's struggle phase (the first 15-20 minutes of focused work) naturally feels uncomfortable before release into absorption occurs. A desensitized dopamine system makes this phase feel unbearable rather than merely effortful. The pull toward high-novelty, low-depth activities becomes neurochemically stronger than the pull toward the work.

The inverse is equally true: a recalibrated (lower, more sensitive) baseline makes deep work feel immediately rewarding. The effort-to-engagement ratio shifts — the mind finds the slow build of understanding satisfying rather than aversive. Simple things yield disproportionate pleasure. The struggle phase becomes survivable.

## Application

Recalibration requires reducing high-stimulation inputs deliberately and consistently — silence, no phone, no media, no novelty for defined periods. The goal is not deprivation but re-sensitization. After even a few days, many people report that previously ordinary activities — conversation, reading, focused work — become noticeably more engaging. The bar has lowered; deep work clears it again.
