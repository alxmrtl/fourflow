# Provisional
*The Maintain quality of Open Mind*

## What it is

When this quality is present, challenge expands rather than contracts you. A hard problem makes you curious. A mistake generates data. A disturbance reveals a pattern worth understanding. When absent, difficulty activates protection: the mind closes, retreats, defends — and the information inside the challenge gets lost to the bracing. The capacity to meet what's hard without immediately classifying it as evidence about yourself is what keeps the mind available for the full complexity of the work.

## Science

Carol Dweck's growth mindset research established the cognitive frame distinction: individuals who understand ability as developable treat difficulty and failure as learning data; those who view ability as fixed treat them as performance signals. The consequences are measurable — growth-oriented individuals persist through challenge, seek feedback, and improve over time; fixed-oriented individuals avoid challenge, hide failure, and plateau.

The neural correlate is the error positivity response (Pe): a brainwave component that appears when people treat mistakes as meaningful information to process. Growth-oriented individuals show stronger Pe responses — they're extracting more signal from errors. Fixed-oriented individuals show attenuated Pe, consistent with defensive disengagement from error information.

For flow specifically, the maintain challenge is that the struggle phase — the first 20-30% of any flow cycle — feels like failure. Without a growth frame, the natural response is to abort at the struggle phase, just before the release into flow that follows it. Provisional keeps the mind open through the difficulty rather than closing around it, which is what makes sustained flow possible across real challenges rather than only easy ones.

## Recall

**Hook:** Every challenge that triggers you is revealing a pattern — and visible patterns can be transformed.
**Mechanism:** Growth orientation reframes difficulty as pattern-revealing information; when you meet challenge with curiosity instead of contraction, each pattern transformed means less friction in future cycles.
**Anchor:** Pattern Literacy — when a disturbance arises, ask "What is this revealing about how I respond?" Name it, log it. The recognition is the first step of transformation.
