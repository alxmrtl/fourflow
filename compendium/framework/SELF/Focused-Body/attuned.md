# Attuned
*The Maintain quality of Focused Body*

## What it is

When this quality is present, the sensory environment is working alongside you rather than against you. The sounds are non-competing, the visual field is task-relevant, the body's proprioceptive signals are settled. When absent, some portion of bandwidth is perpetually resolving conflicts between channels — a distraction over there, an uncomfortable position here, a sound that keeps pulling attention away from where you placed it. The work gets what's left after the sensory environment has taken its share.

## Science

The perceptual aperture operates on a single bandwidth. When multiple sensory channels deliver competing or high-salience information simultaneously, the aperture splits across them rather than concentrating on the task. This isn't inattention — it's correct neural behavior: the brain allocates processing resources to resolve the highest-priority signals in the environment, and conflicting sensory inputs qualify as high-priority by triggering the orienting response.

Sensory gating is the brain's mechanism for filtering irrelevant inputs, but it operates within limits. Chronic high-salience ambient stimulation degrades gating efficiency over time, raising baseline cognitive load without any conscious sense of where the cost is going. The attentional bottleneck effect (Broadbent's filter model) explains why competing channels reduce overall processing capacity: attention is serial at the bottleneck, so input from parallel channels must queue — and the queue itself generates load.

The maintain path is environmental design plus pre-session sensory clearing: identify the highest-competition inputs and resolve them before starting, not mid-session. The window between sessions is when the sensory field can be deliberately shaped.

## Techniques

- [[aperture-absorption]] — narrows perceptual focus to task-relevant sensory channel exclusively
- [[rhythm-and-pacing]] — uses rhythmic physical cues to synchronize body and task engagement
- [[sensory-field-narrowing]] — systematically removes highest-competition sensory inputs before a session

## Recall

**Hook:** When multiple sensory channels compete for attention, the aperture splits across them — no single channel gets full bandwidth, including the task.
**Mechanism:** Reducing conflicting sensory inputs frees the perceptual aperture to concentrate on the task; a coherent sensory environment removes the background processing load of conflict resolution.
**Anchor:** Pre-session sensory scan: What are the three highest-competition inputs right now (visual clutter, uncomfortable seat, background noise)? Resolve at least two before starting.
