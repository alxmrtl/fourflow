# Sensory Coherence

> Reduces conflicting sensory inputs.

## Why It Enables Flow

The perceptual aperture is a single bandwidth — when multiple sensory channels are delivering competing or high-salience information simultaneously, the aperture splits across them rather than concentrating on the task. Sensory coherence is the condition where the sensory environment and the body's sensory state are aligned with and supportive of the work at hand: visual inputs are task-relevant, auditory inputs are non-competing, proprioceptive signals are settled. When sensory channels conflict (visual distraction while trying to focus on text, uncomfortable physical position while trying to enter deep work, competing audio signals), the brain allocates processing resources to resolve the conflict rather than to the task. Sensory coherence removes these competing draws on bandwidth.

## Recall

**Hook:** When multiple sensory channels compete for attention, the aperture splits across them — no single channel gets full bandwidth, including the task.
**Mechanism:** Reducing conflicting sensory inputs frees the perceptual aperture to concentrate on the task; coherent sensory environment removes the background processing load of conflict resolution.
**Anchor:** Pre-session sensory scan: What are the three highest-competition inputs right now (visual clutter, uncomfortable seat, background noise)? Resolve at least two before starting.
