# Attention Mode Toggle

> Deliberately alternate between narrow focused attention and wide open awareness — across visual, auditory, and spatial channels — to prevent the fixation and tunnel vision that fragment embodied presence.

## Protocol

**Visual channel**
1. Focus on one object in front of you for one minute — let it fill your attention.
2. Shift to perceiving all objects in the room simultaneously for one minute — soften focus until no single thing is primary.
3. If outside at night: focus on one star. Then shift to perceiving the entire sky at once.

**Auditory channel**
4. Focus on one nearby sound for one minute — isolate it, let it fill your hearing.
5. Shift to receiving all sounds simultaneously — near, far, high, low — without isolating any.

**Integration**
6. Notice: after the practice, where does your attention naturally rest? Is it strained (over-focused) or lazy (too diffuse)?
7. Make one micro-adjustment — if strained, open slightly; if too diffuse, choose one anchor point.
8. Use this calibrated state to enter work or physical activity.

**Signal recognition**:
- Over-focused → squinting sensation, tunnel vision, tension behind eyes or jaw → open the field
- Under-focused → restlessness, thoughts drifting, no engagement thread → narrow to one anchor

## When to Use

- As a 5-minute recalibration between intense focus blocks
- When body awareness has narrowed to a single point and peripheral presence has collapsed
- Before physical activities requiring spatial awareness (sports, movement practice, performance)
- When you notice attention is either gripping or gone — this resets both

## Key Insight

> "Attention is a lens, not a spotlight. It needs to be able to adjust. If you can only do narrow, you'll strain. If you can only do wide, you'll drift."
