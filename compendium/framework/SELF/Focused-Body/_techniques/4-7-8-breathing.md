# 4-7-8 Breathing

> A specific breath ratio (4 inhale / 7 hold / 8 exhale) that rapidly activates the parasympathetic system — used to reset over-arousal before a work session.

## Mechanism
Extended exhalation activates the vagal brake — the parasympathetic mechanism that decelerates heart rate. Breath retention (the 7-count hold) elevates CO₂, which has a direct calming effect on the amygdala. The extended exhale (8 counts) further amplifies the parasympathetic signal, producing a significant downshift in arousal within 2–3 cycles. The pattern consistently shifts subjective stress ratings and physiological arousal markers within 90 seconds.

## Protocol

1. Exhale completely through the mouth (clear the lungs first).
2. Close the mouth and inhale quietly through the nose for **4 counts**.
3. Hold the breath for **7 counts**.
4. Exhale completely through the mouth for **8 counts**.
5. Repeat for **4 cycles**. No more than 8 in one sitting initially.
6. Note: this is a pre-session tool, not a during-session tool. The extended holds interrupt task engagement.

## When to Use
Pre-session when arriving in a high-stress state — stressed from a prior meeting, anxious about the task ahead, or physiologically wound up from external demands. Not appropriate for under-arousal (it will deepen relaxation, which is counterproductive when you need to energize). Choose resonance frequency breathing or movement instead for under-arousal.
