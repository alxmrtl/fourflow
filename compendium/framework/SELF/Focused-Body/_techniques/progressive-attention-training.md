# Progressive Attention Training

> Build sustained attention the same way you build a muscle — small daily increments, no straining, consistent repetition over 30 days.

## Protocol

1. Sit upright on a chair, motionless — spine supported, hands resting.
2. Soft-focus your gaze on a clock, watch, or timer. Let your eyes settle without squinting or straining — receive the object rather than grip it.
3. When attention drifts (thought, distraction, urge to move), gently return without judgment. Each return is a rep, not a failure.
4. Follow this daily increment schedule:
   - Day 1: 1 minute
   - Day 2: 2 minutes
   - Day 3: 3 minutes
   - Continue adding 1 minute per day
   - Day 30: 30 minutes
5. Practice at the same time each day — morning before other tasks works well.
6. If you miss a day, resume at the previous day's duration, not zero.

**The quality check**: if you're straining or fighting, you're over-threshold. Drop back two days and rebuild from there. Effortful attention is not the goal — sustained receptive attention is.

## When to Use

As a standalone daily practice, separate from work sessions — like a gym session for the attentional system. Not a pre-session warm-up (that's what [[pre-session-clearance]] is for). Best done in the morning before other demands have taxed attentional resources. After 30 days, maintain at 20–30 minutes 3x per week.

## Key Insight

> "You cannot force attention to sustain. You can only practice receiving long enough that sustaining becomes natural."
