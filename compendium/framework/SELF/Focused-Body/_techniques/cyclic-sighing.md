# Cyclic Sighing

> A double inhale through the nose followed by a long exhale — the highest-ROI daily breathing regulation tool for sustained mood and arousal stability.

## Mechanism
A 2023 randomized controlled study (Huberman et al., *Cell Reports Medicine*) compared five respiration protocols and mindfulness meditation. Cyclic sighing — a double inhale through the nose followed by a long exhale — produced the greatest improvements in mood and physiological calm across the day, outperforming all breath patterns and mindfulness in reducing respiratory rate and self-reported anxiety. The double inhale maximizes alveolar inflation (the first inhale opens most alveoli; the top-off recruits collapsed ones), and the extended exhale then deflates the lungs maximally, activating vagal pathways more fully than a single-inhale breath.

## Protocol

**Single-use reset** (1–3 cycles):
1. Inhale fully through the nose.
2. Add a short second inhale through the nose ("top-off" — expand even more).
3. Long, slow exhale through the mouth until lungs are empty.
4. Repeat 1–3 times as needed.

**Daily practice** (5 minutes):
1. Perform the single-use protocol continuously for 5 minutes.
2. Best done in the morning to establish mood and arousal baseline for the day.
3. Research shows 5 minutes outperformed longer mindfulness sessions for mood improvement and anxiety reduction.

**Mid-session use**: A single cycle (one double inhale + full exhale) at any natural pause point maintains regulation without breaking task engagement.

## When to Use
As a daily morning practice for baseline mood and arousal regulation. As a mid-session reset (single cycle) when arousal starts to drift. Before any high-stakes performance moment. Especially useful when 4-7-8 feels too intense or when the over-arousal is mild to moderate.

## Key Insight
> "One deliberate sigh is enough to shift your nervous system. The body already knows how to regulate — cyclic sighing just amplifies what it does naturally."
