# Breath Regulation

> Three breath tools matched to situation: cyclic sighing for daily baseline and mild reset, resonance frequency breathing for sustained pre-session regulation, 4-7-8 for acute over-arousal — all operating through the same vagal mechanism.

## Mechanism

The breath is the fastest bidirectional lever on the nervous system because it's the only autonomic function under voluntary control. Extended exhalation activates the vagal brake — decelerating heart rate and shifting sympathetic/parasympathetic balance. Slow breathing at ~0.1 Hz (5–6 breaths/minute) creates cardiac-respiratory resonance, maximizing HRV — the measurable signature of optimal autonomic balance. Cyclic sighing (double inhale + full exhale) maximizes alveolar inflation on inhale, then maximizes CO₂ offload on exhale, activating vagal pathways more fully than single-inhale patterns. All three techniques work through the same pathway; they differ in depth, duration, and appropriate arousal state.

## Protocol

**Tool 1 — Cyclic Sighing** (daily baseline + mild in-session reset)
- Inhale fully through the nose
- Add a short second inhale through the nose ("top-off")
- Long, slow exhale through the mouth until lungs are empty
- For daily practice: 5 minutes in the morning (outperforms longer mindfulness sessions for mood and anxiety in RCT, Huberman et al. 2023)
- For mid-session: 1 cycle at any natural pause point

**Tool 2 — Resonance Frequency Breathing** (pre-session regulation + mid-session maintenance)
- Breathe diaphragmatically: belly expands on inhale, not chest
- 5 seconds inhale / 5 seconds exhale (~5.5 bpm) as the default target
- Tune for state: over-aroused → 4s in / 6s out; under-aroused → 6s in / 4s out
- 5 minutes shifts HRV measurably; 10 minutes produces stronger effects
- During sessions: 60 seconds at natural pause points (between sub-goals, before difficult decisions)

**Tool 3 — 4-7-8 Breathing** (acute over-arousal reset only)
- Exhale completely through the mouth to clear the lungs
- Inhale through nose: 4 counts
- Hold: 7 counts (CO₂ elevation calms amygdala directly)
- Exhale through mouth: 8 counts
- 4 cycles; shifts arousal within 90 seconds
- Pre-session only — the holds interrupt task engagement

**Matching tool to state:**
| State | Tool |
|-------|------|
| Daily mood/arousal baseline | Cyclic sighing (5 min) |
| Pre-session regulation | Resonance frequency (5–10 min) |
| Mild mid-session drift | Cyclic sighing (1 cycle) |
| Over-aroused pre-session | 4-7-8 (4 cycles), then resonance frequency |
| Under-arousal | Movement primer instead (breath won't energize) |

## When to Use

Before any focused work session (resonance frequency as default). On arrival in a high-stress state (4-7-8 first, then resonance). As a daily morning practice (cyclic sighing). At natural pause points mid-session to maintain regulation (cyclic sighing or resonance).
