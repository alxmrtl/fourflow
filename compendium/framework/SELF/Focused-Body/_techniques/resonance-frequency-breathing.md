# Resonance Frequency Breathing

> Slow-paced diaphragmatic breathing at ~5.5 breaths per minute to create cardiac-respiratory resonance — maximizing HRV and the optimal autonomic balance where flow lives.

## Mechanism
Breathing rate controls the sympathetic/parasympathetic balance through the vagus nerve — the primary parasympathetic pathway. Slow breathing at approximately 0.1 Hz (5–6 breaths per minute, or "resonance frequency") creates a resonance effect between respiratory and cardiovascular oscillations that maximizes HRV. HRV is the measurable signature of optimal autonomic balance: flexible, responsive nervous system regulation. Research (Laborde et al., 2022; Lehrer & Gevirtz, 2014) shows 4–8 weeks of regular practice produces lasting HRV increases, reduced anxiety, and improved performance under pressure. The acute effect — shifting arousal within minutes — is accessible immediately on the first use.

## Protocol

1. **Find your resonance**: Most adults resonate at 5–6 breaths per minute. Start with 5 seconds inhale / 5 seconds exhale.
2. **Breathe diaphragmatically**: The belly expands on inhale, not the chest. This activates vagal branches running through the diaphragm, directly stimulating parasympathetic response.
3. **Tune the ratio for state**:
   - Over-aroused: 4 seconds inhale / 6 seconds exhale (slightly parasympathetic-biased)
   - Under-aroused: 6 seconds inhale / 4 seconds exhale (slightly sympathetic-biased)
   - Well-regulated target: equal ratio at ~5.5 bpm
4. **Duration**: 5 minutes before a session shifts HRV measurably. 10 minutes produces stronger effects.
5. **During sessions**: 60-second resonance breaths at natural pause points (between sub-goals, before difficult decisions) maintain regulation without breaking flow.

## When to Use
As the primary pre-session physiological regulation tool. Also as a mid-session reset when the body signals arousal drift. As a daily practice (10 minutes, any time) to build resting HRV baseline over weeks.
