# Body-State Check

> A 60-second arousal assessment before beginning focused work — identifies whether you're over-aroused, under-aroused, or well-regulated, then matches the appropriate tool to the state.

## Mechanism
Many people begin work sessions in the wrong arousal zone and spend 20–30 minutes of the session "warming up" into regulation. The 60-second check eliminates this waste by externalizing the diagnostic before sitting down. The body announces its state clearly through heart rate, breath quality, muscle tension, and mental activity — reading these signals consciously converts an automatic, unmanaged process into a deliberate one that costs 60 seconds and saves the first 30 minutes of session quality.

## Protocol

**Step 1 — Scan four signals**:
1. **Heart rate**: Elevated or racing? (over-aroused) Flat or slow? (under-aroused) Steady, moderate? (well-regulated)
2. **Breath quality**: Shallow, in the chest? (over-aroused) Slow, easy, natural? (well-regulated) Heavy, yawning? (under-aroused)
3. **Muscle tension**: Jaw, shoulders, hands gripped? (over-aroused) Relaxed, loose? (well-regulated)
4. **Mental activity**: Racing, jumping between thoughts? (over-aroused) Clear and present? (well-regulated) Foggy, slow? (under-aroused)

**Step 2 — Match tool to state**:
- **Over-aroused** → [[4-7-8-breathing]] (acute reset) OR [[cyclic-sighing]] (gentler) OR [[resonance-frequency-breathing]] with extended exhale ratio
- **Under-aroused** → brief aerobic burst (10 jumping jacks, short walk), cold water on face, [[resonance-frequency-breathing]] with slightly longer inhale
- **Well-regulated** → begin work immediately

## When to Use
Before every focused work session. Also mid-session when you notice something has shifted — focus is fragmenting, motivation is dropping, or the body feels different from when you started. Takes 60 seconds. Run it as a gate, not an afterthought.

## Key Insight
> "The body tells you where it is. Check before you sit. Regulate before you start."
