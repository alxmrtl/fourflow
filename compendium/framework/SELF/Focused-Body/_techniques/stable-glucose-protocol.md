# Stable Glucose Protocol

> A meal timing and composition strategy that maintains consistent brain fuel availability during flow sessions — converting glucose from volatile peaks into steady supply.

## Mechanism
Glucose is the brain's primary fuel, but stable supply matters more than total quantity. Glycemic spikes followed by crashes create predictable concentration windows that collapse mid-session. The prefrontal cortex — the seat of cognitive flexibility and the region whose activity patterns during flow — is exquisitely sensitive to glucose availability. Stable fuel maintains stable aperture width. Caffeine timing interacts with this: adenosine (the tiredness signal) builds during waking hours, and caffeine blocks its receptors temporarily. Delaying coffee 90–120 minutes after waking allows adenosine to clear naturally first, producing a cleaner boost without the sharp afternoon rebound.

## Protocol

**Pre-session meal (60–90 minutes before, not immediately before)**:
- **Composition**: protein + fat + slow-release carbohydrates (vegetables, legumes, whole grains) rather than refined carbohydrates alone
- **Avoid**: high-glycemic meals immediately before sessions — the insulin response can paradoxically reduce available glucose 45–60 minutes later
- **Fasting sessions**: If working in a fasted state (morning), ensure the prior night's meal was adequate and consider a small amount of glucose (fruit) if the session exceeds 90 minutes

**During sessions longer than 2 hours**:
- Small amounts of glucose (fruit, complex carbohydrate) can maintain levels without an insulin spike
- Avoid sugary drinks or refined snacks — the spike-crash cycle is worse mid-session than low fuel

**Caffeine timing**:
- Delay first coffee 90–120 minutes after waking
- This allows natural adenosine clearing before blocking receptors
- Result: cleaner, stronger, longer-lasting boost and reduced afternoon energy crash

## When to Use
As standard pre-session preparation. Most relevant for morning flow sessions (following the longest fasting window) and for sessions following high-glycemic meals. The caffeine timing protocol applies daily regardless of session schedule.

## Key Insight
> "What you eat before a flow session determines whether you're working at full bandwidth or borrowing against a debt that crashes mid-session."
