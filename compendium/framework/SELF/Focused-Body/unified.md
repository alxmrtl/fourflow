# Unified
*The Concentrate quality of Focused Body*

## What it is

When this quality is present, there's no gap between what you mean to do and what the body is doing. The execution happens without being watched, corrected, or narrated by a mind standing apart from it. When absent, you can feel the split — thinking about what you're doing while doing it, monitoring the output while producing it, holding intention in tension with action rather than releasing into it. That split is the leak. Every cycle of monitoring is a cycle that isn't in the work.

## Science

The Default Mode Network (DMN) is the self-referential brain network that generates internal narrative — the "observer" layer that comments on experience rather than inhabiting it. For flow to occur, DMN activity must quiet and the Task-Positive Network (TPN) must take over: external engagement replaces internal monitoring. These networks are anti-correlated; activating one suppresses the other.

Arne Dietrich's transient hypofrontality hypothesis explains the mechanism: sustained physical or cognitive effort temporarily reduces prefrontal cortex activity, which is responsible for self-monitoring and explicit processing. When prefrontal load drops, the monitoring layer goes quiet and embodied presence becomes possible. The subjective experience is the closing of the mind-body gap — action and awareness merge because the mechanism generating the separation has been suppressed.

What disrupts this is attention allocated to the monitoring layer itself: checking progress, evaluating performance, managing identity. Each check-in reasserts the observer and re-opens the gap. The concentrate path is structural: remove what the monitor would need to track before starting, then commit to zero check-ins until the session ends.

## Recall

**Hook:** When what the mind intends and what the body is doing are misaligned, attention splits between the two signals — flow cannot build across a split.
**Mechanism:** Coherence between intention and physical execution removes internal friction, freeing the full bandwidth of awareness for the task.
**Anchor:** Before starting: name the intention ("I'm here to move section 2 forward"), then scan the body briefly (jaw, shoulders, breath) and release what's held. Begin from alignment.
