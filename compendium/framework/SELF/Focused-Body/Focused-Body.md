# Focused Body

> The somatic attention layer — using the body's sensory field as the primary anchor for awareness, collapsing the mind-body gap so action is felt rather than thought.

## Why It Enables Flow

Flow requires attention to narrow completely onto the present task. The body's sensory field is the most direct anchor for that narrowing — it is already located in the present moment where the work actually happens. When attention drops into physical sensation, the Default Mode Network (self-referential thought) quiets, and the Task-Positive Network activates. This shift is not metaphor; it is a measurable neurological state change.

The failure mode is disembodiment: mind projected forward into outcomes or backward into analysis while the body runs on autopilot. This gap is experienced as tension, anxiety, and the particular kind of paralysis where you know what to do but can't start. Self-referential chatter fills the space. The body becomes noise rather than signal — and flow becomes inaccessible from inside that noise.
