# Problem Space Index

> **How to use**: Match the presenting problem to the closest category. Each entry lists the mechanics most relevant to that problem, with a brief note on *why* each applies. Follow the links to find specific techniques. A problem often maps to mechanics across multiple pillars — that's intentional.
>
> **For agents**: When a user presents a problem, match it to the closest category here first. Then read the mechanic files listed to find applicable techniques. Do not assume the problem maps to only one pillar.

## Emotional State

### Anxiety before starting / anticipatory dread
*Can't bring yourself to begin. Worried about what might happen. Needing certainty before moving.*
- [[anxiety-suppression]] — the primary mechanic; re-patterning transforms the underlying pattern driving the dread ✓
- [[psychological-safety]] — the open-heart state that anxiety blocks; unconditional trust is the replacement quality ✓
- [[outcome-detachment]] — the dread is often attachment to a specific outcome; detachment removes the threat ✓
- [[challenge-skill-calibration]] — sometimes the anxiety is accurate: the task may be genuinely beyond current skill range
- [[ego-monitoring-removal]] — the dread may be fear of evaluation, not fear of failure

### Emotional reactivity / can't regulate feelings
*Emotions spiking unexpectedly. Reactions feel disproportionate. Getting pulled out of flow by feeling.*
- [[anxiety-suppression]] — re-patterning is the core practice for transforming reactive patterns ✓
- [[emotional-neutrality]] — the mechanic specifically for preventing affective spikes from hijacking attention
- [[psychological-safety]] — heart-closed state amplifies reactivity; restoring open heart restores stability ✓
- [[physiological-regulation]] — body-level dysregulation often precedes emotional reactivity

### Emotionally attached to outcome or object / can't let go
*Holding too tight to a result, possession, or idea. Disproportionate distress when threatened. Identity wrapped up in something external.*
- [[outcome-detachment]] — the primary mechanic; capital-L letting go through pattern transformation ✓
- [[ego-monitoring-removal]] — the attachment is often the ego using the object/outcome as a self-symbol; removal of that monitoring dissolves the grip
- [[cognitive-load-reduction]] — possession-as-identity audit: which objects are driving vigilance? ✓
- [[anxiety-suppression]] — the emotional charge underneath attachment; re-patterning releases it ✓
- [[psychological-safety]] — unconditional well-being is what makes it safe to release; safety doesn't depend on getting the thing ✓

### Going through the motions / no genuine engagement
*Performing the work but not present to it. Mechanical execution. Disconnected.*
- [[effort-reward-coupling]] — coupling between effort and intrinsic reward may have broken
- [[action-awareness-merging]] — the feeling is absence of this; action and awareness have split
- [[psychological-safety]] — performing vs. being is the closed-heart signature ✓
- [[identity-immersion]] — insufficient narrative absorption in the role or story
- [[ignited-curiosity / intrinsic-reward-signaling]] — the intrinsic pull has gone quiet

### Self-conscious / monitoring yourself while performing
*Watching yourself do the work. Inner critic running commentary. Performing for an imagined audience.*
- [[ego-monitoring-removal]] — the primary mechanic; this is exactly what it targets
- [[psychological-safety]] — self-consciousness is the closed-heart state (performing rather than being) ✓
- [[outcome-detachment]] — monitoring is the ego checking whether the outcome is on track ✓
- [[action-awareness-merging]] — the merge is broken when you're watching yourself

### Overwhelmed / everything feels too much
*Too many demands, inputs, or decisions. Cognitive and emotional system maxed out.*
- [[cognitive-load-reduction]] — assess total cognitive overhead from possessions, open loops, and environment ✓
- [[overchoice-elimination]] — too many options consuming decision bandwidth
- [[attention-residue-elimination]] — prior tasks bleeding in, adding to the load
- [[compression-over-expansion]] — the solution is compression, not management
- [[priority-singularity]] — overwhelm often signals the absence of a single clear priority
- [[anxiety-suppression]] — overwhelm frequently has an emotional/pattern component ✓

### Feeling stuck / resistance to starting
*Something is blocking entry. Procrastinating. Doing everything except the thing.*
- [[anxiety-suppression]] — resistance often has an anticipatory fear at its root ✓
- [[ego-monitoring-removal]] — fear of evaluation or failure of performance
- [[challenge-skill-calibration]] — task may be too far outside current skill (real mismatch, not just fear) ✓
- [[psychological-safety]] — open heart makes the first move feel safe ✓
- [[clear-goals]] — sometimes resistance is the absence of a clear, concrete starting point ✓
- [[stakes-definition]] — when stakes aren't visceral, the cost of not starting feels lower than the cost of starting ✓

## Cognitive / Attention

### Can't focus / attention keeps fragmenting
*Mind pulls away from the task. Attention splitting. Distraction winning.*
- [[cognitive-load-reduction]] — check total cognitive overhead: possessions, environment, open loops ✓
- [[attention-residue-elimination]] — prior tasks still occupying working memory
- [[interruption-control]] — external interruptions breaking the session
- [[environmental-simplicity]] — physical environment generating background processing load ✓
- [[overchoice-elimination]] — too many options consuming bandwidth
- [[physiological-regulation]] — body may be dysregulated, pulling attention to itself

### Mind won't quiet / thoughts keep intruding
*Racing thoughts. Rumination. Inner noise that won't stop.*
- [[anxiety-suppression]] — intrusive thoughts often have an anxious pattern underneath; DMN is the neurological substrate of rumination — goal engagement suppresses it in real time ✓
- [[clear-goals]] — a specific goal triggers PFC takeover and suppresses DMN drift; fastest real-time lever when re-patterning isn't available ✓
- [[attention-residue-elimination]] — open loops and unfinished items generating thought-intrusions
- [[psychological-safety]] — heart-closed state generates monitoring thoughts ✓
- [[environmental-simplicity]] — external noise translated into internal noise ✓
- [[cognitive-load-reduction]] — total overhead has exceeded processing capacity ✓

### Too many options / decision paralysis
*Can't choose. Circling the same options. Mental bandwidth consumed by selection.*
- [[overchoice-elimination]] — the primary mechanic
- [[priority-singularity]] — the absence of a clear hierarchy is the root cause
- [[compression-over-expansion]] — solution is to reduce the option set, not evaluate more
- [[cognitive-load-reduction]] — decision load is a major cognitive overhead source ✓

### Carrying too many open loops / attention residue
*Unfinished tasks bleeding into focus. Can't fully arrive at the current work.*
- [[attention-residue-elimination]] — the primary mechanic; capturing and closing open loops
- [[cognitive-load-reduction]] — open loops are a direct cognitive load source ✓
- [[clear-goals]] — open loops often persist because the completion criteria aren't defined

### Unclear what to do next / goals feel fuzzy
*Direction is vague. Can't identify the concrete next action. Work feels abstract.*
- [[clear-goals]] — primary mechanic; specificity of goal drives specificity of action; goal hierarchy (big for direction, small for clarity) resolves the mismatch ✓
- [[priority-singularity]] — multiple competing goals creating diffuse focus
- [[outcome-horizon-setting]] — holding the goal at the wrong time horizon
- [[stakes-definition]] — when stakes aren't clear, urgency collapses and goals feel optional ✓

## Body / Energy

### Low energy / can't sustain effort
*Physically depleted. Output degrading over the session. Running on empty.*
- [[energy-sufficiency]] — the primary mechanic for baseline physiological resource management
- [[rhythm-and-pacing]] — sustainable effort requires rhythm, not linear output
- [[physiological-regulation]] — body state may be contributing to energy drain
- [[challenge-skill-calibration]] — effort that exceeds skill range depletes energy faster

### Actions feel mechanical / no body-mind connection
*Movement or work feels disconnected. Body and mind operating separately.*
- [[action-awareness-merging]] — the primary mechanic; merging is its definition; when fully absorbed, no bandwidth remains for the split ✓
- [[mind-body-coherence]] — the underlying alignment between body signals and cognitive state
- [[focused-body / sensory-coherence]] — sensory engagement as the bridge between body and attention
- [[physiological-regulation]] — body may be in a dysregulated state that breaks the connection

### Rhythm broken / can't find sustainable pace
*Starting and stopping. Burning bright then crashing. No sustainable work cadence.*
- [[rhythm-and-pacing]] — the primary mechanic
- [[energy-sufficiency]] — rhythm depends on having adequate resources to sustain it
- [[tension-release-dynamics]] — flow has a natural tension/release cycle; forcing it breaks the rhythm
- [[challenge-skill-calibration]] — mismatch between task demand and capacity disrupts natural pacing

## Environment / Tools

### Physical environment feels chaotic / can't settle
*Clutter, noise, or visual complexity making it hard to drop in. Space feels like it's working against you.*
- [[environmental-simplicity]] — the primary mechanic; reducing background cognitive noise ✓
- [[physical-space-priming]] — intentional design of the entry into focus work
- [[interruption-control]] — managing external signal sources
- [[sensory-coherence]] — environmental inputs should support rather than fragment sensory attention
- [[cognitive-load-reduction]] — physical environment directly contributes to cognitive overhead ✓

### Too much friction in the workflow
*Tools fighting you. Steps that should be automatic requiring effort. Workflow creating resistance.*
- [[friction-removal]] — the primary mechanic
- [[tool-task-alignment]] — tools may be mismatched to the specific task
- [[automation-of-non-creative-steps]] — non-creative steps consuming creative bandwidth
- [[subtraction-beats-addition]] — the solution is usually removing friction sources, not adding tools ✓

### Getting too many interruptions
*External demands breaking focus. Constant re-entry cost.*
- [[interruption-control]] — the primary mechanic; structuring for uninterrupted blocks
- [[social-field-alignment]] — social environment not aligned with focus needs
- [[attention-residue-elimination]] — each interruption leaves residue that compounds

### Tools feeling wrong for the task
*Using the right tool for the wrong job, or vice versa. Mismatch between instrument and intent.*
- [[tool-task-alignment]] — the primary mechanic
- [[friction-removal]] — misalignment creates friction as a symptom
- [[subtraction-beats-addition]] — often the solution is removing a tool, not adding one ✓

## Direction / Narrative

### Lost sense of purpose / why am I doing this?
*The work feels disconnected from anything that matters. Going through motions without meaning.*
- [[meaning-coherence]] — coherence between the work and a larger story
- [[grounding-values / values-integration]] — the work may be misaligned with actual values
- [[stakes-definition]] — without clear stakes, purpose evaporates
- [[identity-immersion]] — insufficient absorption in a meaningful narrative frame

### No momentum / stalled in the story
*Work is happening but not building toward anything. Sessions aren't compounding. No narrative arc.*
- [[narrative-momentum]] — the primary mechanic; time as navigation not weather; the mirror delay as narrative fuel ✓
- [[progress-visibility]] — momentum requires seeing progress; if it's invisible, it doesn't feel real
- [[tension-release-dynamics]] — flow has a story structure; identifying where in the arc you are
- [[commitment-escalation]] — stakes may need to increase to reinvigorate forward motion
- [[meaning-coherence]] — when meaning fragments, narrative fragments with it; coherence holds the arc together ✓

### Role feels unclear or disempowered
*Unclear who I am in this situation. Not sure I have authority or ownership. Identity doesn't fit.*
- [[role-clarity]] — the primary mechanic; clear definition of your role and its domain
- [[responsibility-without-evaluation-pressure]] — ownership without the performance monitoring that blocks flow
- [[identity-alignment]] — the role may be misaligned with deeper identity
- [[empowered-role / commitment-escalation]] — sometimes lack of commitment is the signal

### Goals feel abstract / no clear target
*Working toward something vague. Can't visualize what "done" looks like.*
- [[clear-goals]] — specificity of the target ✓
- [[outcome-horizon-setting]] — goals may be held at the wrong time horizon; match horizon to current aperture width ✓
- [[stakes-definition]] — abstract goals typically lack consequences that make them feel real ✓
- [[priority-singularity]] — competing goals making each feel abstract by dilution

### Time feels wrong / distorted / adversarial
*Time dragging unbearably, or vanishing without progress. Feeling like time is happening to you rather than something you're navigating.*
- [[action-awareness-merging]] — full aperture absorption eliminates the self-referential loop that generates the experience of time dragging ✓
- [[anxiety-suppression]] — anxiety narrows the perceptual aperture; each moment delivers less reality, making seconds feel impoverished ✓
- [[outcome-horizon-setting]] — the horizon may be mismatched to current aperture capacity; narrow when stressed, widen when clear ✓
- [[narrative-momentum]] — time as navigation, not weather; generating story-level forward motion restores agency over temporal experience ✓
- [[meaning-coherence]] — incoherent meaning fragments the aperture into disconnected moments; coherence makes time feel continuous ✓
- [[meaning-stabilization]] — prevents the aperture from collapsing between high-engagement periods ✓
- [[outcome-detachment]] — attachment narrows the aperture (staring at the mirror); detachment widens it (turning back to the work) ✓

## Spirit / Meaning

### Values conflict / feeling inauthentic
*Work is misaligned with what actually matters. Internal friction from integrity gap.*
- [[values-integration]] — the primary mechanic; aligning action with values
- [[integrity-loops]] — closing the loop between stated values and actual behavior ✓
- [[internal-alignment]] — the broader coherence between inner state and outer action
- [[grounding-values / meaning-stabilization]] — stabilizing on what's actually true for you

### Captured by a narrative / trusting the wrong thing
*Committed to a person, framework, ideology, or self-story that isn't actually serving you. Justifying rather than seeing. Can't tell if what you trust is real.*
- [[integrity-loops]] — calibrated trust (pistis): reality-indexed trust is the foundation; discernment is constitutive — trust without calibration is credulity ✓
- [[values-integration]] — captured narratives typically involve values that have been adopted from outside rather than grounded in what's actually true
- [[internal-alignment]] — the gap between what you're committed to and what you're observing in reality
- [[outcome-detachment]] — attachment to the trusted thing can make it impossible to see it clearly ✓
- [[meaning-stabilization]] — if meaning depends on the framework being right, losing it feels existential

### Curiosity flat / nothing feels interesting
*Everything is effort. The pull isn't there. Work feels like pushing a rock.*
- [[intrinsic-reward-signaling]] — the signal that makes engagement self-reinforcing has gone quiet; neurochemical stacking (curiosity→passion→purpose) re-ignites from the ground up ✓
- [[exploration-bias]] — novelty and exploration are curiosity's fuel; may need new inputs
- [[curiosity-loops]] — the mechanism that generates and sustains curious engagement
- [[challenge-skill-calibration]] — too easy generates boredom, which kills curiosity ✓
- [[playfulness]] — curiosity often returns through play before it returns through purpose

### Vision unclear / can't see where this leads
*No compelling future pulling you forward. Long-range attractor has gone dark.*
- [[vision-priming]] — the anti-vision contrast technique; vision as trajectory selection through pre-existing structure ✓
- [[long-horizon-coherence]] — trajectory consistency as manifestation; coherent direction across forks determines which branch of reality you navigate into ✓
- [[inspirational-compression]] — distilling the vision into something vivid and portable
- [[symbolic-meaning]] — abstract visions need concrete symbols to remain activatable
- [[stakes-definition]] — a vision without felt stakes has no urgency; the anti-vision protocol loads both simultaneously ✓

### Vision is clear but feels hollow / not actually motivating
*You can describe the goal but it doesn't pull. Going through the motions of having a vision without feeling it.*
- [[vision-priming]] — check whether the vision is abundance-native or scarcity-derived; many compelling visions are organized around fleeing rather than arriving ✓
- [[meaning-coherence]] — the vision may be disconnected from a larger story that makes it feel real
- [[values-integration]] — the vision may be misaligned with actual values (aspirational vs. authentic)
- [[identity-immersion]] — insufficient absorption in the narrative frame that gives the vision meaning
- [[psychological-safety]] — closed-heart state makes genuine desire hard to access; vision feels like obligation ✓

### Meaning absent / work feels empty
*Technically functioning but no sense that any of it matters.*
- [[meaning-coherence]] — the coherence layer between work and a larger story
- [[meaning-stabilization]] — anchoring meaning so it doesn't fluctuate with conditions; purpose has measurable physiological effects (2x mortality difference) — it's a biological regulator, not optional fuel ✓
- [[values-integration]] — emptiness often signals values-behavior misalignment
- [[identity-immersion]] — meaning often requires sufficient absorption in a narrative frame to feel real
- [[clear-goals]] — a goal gives the brain a directed attractor; without one, neural energy defaults inward
- [[psychological-safety]] — conditional well-being blocks access to intrinsic meaning ✓

## Cross-Pillar Patterns

*These are the patterns that span multiple dimensions simultaneously — often the hardest to diagnose because they present differently on different days.*

### Fear of failure / paralysis around making mistakes
*Avoiding attempts because failure feels too costly. Doing safe work instead of important work. Knowing what to do but not doing it.*
- [[error-tolerance]] — the primary mechanic; reframes failure as portfolio investment rather than identity verdict ✓
- [[psychological-safety]] — unconditional well-being removes identity stakes from failure outcomes ✓
- [[outcome-detachment]] — attachment to a specific result makes failure feel like a self-threat ✓
- [[challenge-skill-calibration]] — sometimes the fear is accurate signal: the task is genuinely beyond current skill; decompose rather than push ✓
- [[ego-monitoring-removal]] — fear of failure is often fear of evaluation; the monitoring loop, not the task difficulty

### Performing well below known capability
*You know you can do better but can't access it. The gap between ceiling and floor is wide.*
- [[anxiety-suppression]] — patterns blocking access to capability ✓
- [[ego-monitoring-removal]] — evaluation pressure suppressing natural performance
- [[challenge-skill-calibration]] — skill-challenge ratio may be off in either direction
- [[action-awareness-merging]] — the integration required for peak performance is broken
- [[psychological-safety]] — the open-heart state is the prerequisite for accessing full capability ✓

### Deep resistance to a specific thing (not general)
*One task, person, or domain generates strong avoidance while everything else is fine.*
- [[anxiety-suppression]] — targeted pattern associated with that specific context ✓
- [[outcome-detachment]] — specific attachment or threat associated with that domain ✓
- [[ego-monitoring-removal]] — specific evaluation context triggering the monitoring loop
- [[values-integration]] — the resistance may be accurate: the thing violates actual values

### High output but low satisfaction
*Getting a lot done but it doesn't feel good. Productivity without fulfillment.*
- [[effort-reward-coupling]] — the coupling between effort and intrinsic reward has broken
- [[outcome-detachment]] — satisfaction contingent on outcomes that keep shifting ✓
- [[meaning-coherence]] — output is disconnected from the larger story
- [[psychological-safety]] — conditional well-being means satisfaction is always deferred ✓
- [[grounding-values]] — activity is misaligned with what actually matters
