# Covenant vs. Authority Audit

> Distinguishes whether you're operating from identity-level commitment (covenant) or from what merely feels right or permissible (authority) — because the gap between these two is where alignment breaks under pressure.

## Mechanism
Decisions can be made at different levels. Two levels that are easily confused: L1 (authority — what feels right or permissible) and L3 (covenant — identity-level commitments that bind across time). Authority is responsive to conditions: it shifts with mood, energy, social context, and felt sense. Covenant is structural: it holds regardless of conditions because it's woven into identity. When someone acts from authority believing they're acting from covenant, they experience bewildering inconsistency — "I was so clear about this yesterday, why can't I hold it today?" The answer: yesterday's clarity was a felt-sense state (L1), not an identity structure (L3). The audit separates the two so you can build the real thing rather than repeatedly losing the simulation.

## Protocol

1. **Identify the commitment or stance.** What are you acting from right now? What do you believe is driving this decision?
2. **Test for L1 (authority).** Ask:
   - Does this shift with mood or energy? (If yes → L1)
   - Would a different social context change this? (If yes → L1)
   - Is this based on what *feels right* rather than what *is right for who I am*? (If yes → L1)
   - Signal phrase: "This feels right"
3. **Test for L3 (covenant).** Ask:
   - Does this hold across contexts, even uncomfortable ones? (If yes → L3)
   - Would I sustain this even when it doesn't feel good? (If yes → L3)
   - Is this about who I am, not about what I prefer? (If yes → L3)
   - Signal phrase: "This is who I am"
4. **Name the actual operating layer.** Without judgment. L1 is not wrong — it's just not covenant. Much of life operates perfectly well at L1. The problem is only when L1 is mistaken for L3.
5. **If L1 and you need L3**: The gap is not willpower. It's integration. The commitment needs to move from "something I believe" to "something I am." This happens through repeated action that builds identity evidence, not through more intense declaration.

## When to Use
When you notice inconsistency in your own behaviour across contexts. When a commitment felt completely solid yesterday but shaky today. When someone's words and actions don't match (including your own). When deciding whether to escalate commitment — first verify the base commitment is covenant, not authority.

## Key Insight
> "Authority shifts with conditions; covenant holds across them. Most alignment failures are L1 being mistaken for L3."
