# Act From Worth

> Take every action from the felt position of already being whole — so your actions are expressions of fullness rather than attempts to earn, prove, or justify your existence.

## Protocol

**Morning Decision** (before the day assigns you a worth-level):
Before your feet hit the floor, before checking messages, before engaging with any external signal of how you're performing:
1. **Decide your baseline** — not affirm, not hope, DECIDE. "Today I am already enough. My worth is not determined by today's output." Say it once, mean it, move on
2. **Set the action frame** — "Everything I do today is overflow. I act because I have something to give, not because I need to prove I'm worth having here"
3. **Note the difference** — actions taken from overflow feel different: lighter, more generous, less monitored. When you notice tightness returning, that's the earning pattern re-engaging. Name it, return to baseline

**In-session check** (when evaluation pressure spikes):
Ask: "Am I doing this to express, or to prove?" If proving: pause, name the evaluator (real or imagined), ask whether their assessment of this output actually determines your worth. It doesn't. Return to the work.

## When to Use

- Before any high-stakes output (presentation, pitch, creative submission, difficult conversation)
- When self-editing is running hotter than necessary — over-polishing as proof-seeking
- When procrastination is really avoidance of the evaluation that completion would invite
- When comparing output to others' work is fragmenting attention
- As a daily orientation practice for sustained creative work where evaluation pressure compounds

## Key Insight

> "Act because you're already whole and your actions are overflow, not compensation. The shift from toward-worth to from-worth is the shift from audition to expression."
