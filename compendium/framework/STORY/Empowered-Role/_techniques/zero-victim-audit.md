# Zero Victim Audit

> Victim narratives don't require dramatic injustice — even subtle ones drain the sense that your actions matter.

## Protocol
1. Write a brief narrative of a current difficulty or a recent painful outcome — 5–10 sentences, no filter.
2. Scan for passive constructions and external attributions: anywhere you are "acted upon," "affected by," "blocked by," "unable to because of," or where causation is assigned to things outside you.
3. For each: ask "What was my role in this? What did I do or not do that contributed?" This is not about blame — it is about recovering the part of the causal picture that involves your choices.
4. Rewrite the narrative in active voice with yourself as a present causal agent. The external factors can remain — they just can't be the only causes.
5. For any item where you genuinely had no causal role: that's a legitimate external constraint, not a victim narrative. Leave it as-is. The audit distinguishes between the two.
6. Run this audit monthly on any narrative that has felt stuck or hopeless.

## When to Use
- When a narrative about a difficulty has been held for more than two weeks without producing any new action
- When you notice that thinking about a problem produces helplessness rather than options
- Before any significant decision-making about whether to continue, change, or exit a situation — the audit ensures the decision is made from full agency rather than from passive constraint

## Key Insight
> "You don't have to deny what happened to you. You just have to find where you were also a cause."
