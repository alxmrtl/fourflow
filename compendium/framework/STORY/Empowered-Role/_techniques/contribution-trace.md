# Contribution Trace

> Abstract work feels meaningless because the chain from your output to its human impact is invisible — trace it.

## Protocol
1. Name your primary current output: what are you actually producing? (Not your role title — the specific thing that comes out of your work.)
2. Trace step one: who receives this output directly? Name or describe them specifically.
3. Trace step two: what does that person do with your output? How does it serve their work or goals?
4. Trace step three: who does that person serve, and how does your output — via them — serve those end people?
5. Write the full chain in one paragraph. Read it before beginning work sessions on the relevant project.
6. Update the trace when the work changes significantly, or quarterly as a standing practice.

## When to Use
- When work has become routinized and feels like production rather than contribution
- Before large output-generation sessions (writing, building, designing) where motivation needs anchoring
- During difficult stretches of a project where the human impact feels distant and irrelevant

## Key Insight
> "Every abstract output is a human act at the end of a chain. Trace the chain and the act becomes real again."
