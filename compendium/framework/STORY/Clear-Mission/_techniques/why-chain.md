# Why Chain

> Surface missions are proxies. Chase the chain down until you hit bedrock — the reason that needs no further reason.

## Protocol
1. State your current mission in one sentence.
2. Ask: "Why does this matter to me?" Write the first honest answer.
3. Ask of that answer: "Why does that matter to me?" Write again.
4. Repeat at least 5 times — more if needed. Don't stop when you hit something that sounds good; stop when the next "why" produces no coherent answer or produces "because that's just who I am / what I care about."
5. That final answer is the bedrock. Write it in 1–2 sentences.
6. Now rewrite your mission statement so it explicitly connects to the bedrock: "I am [mission] because it is the current best path to [bedrock]."
7. When the specific mission becomes difficult, return to the bedrock — the question is not "do I want to keep doing this?" but "is this still the path to what I actually care about?"

## When to Use
- When motivation for a long-standing mission has declined — often signals the instrumental chain is weakening while the bedrock remains intact
- Before committing to any major multi-month project — to ensure it connects to something that will sustain it
- When someone challenges your mission and you find yourself unable to articulate why it matters beyond "it just seems like the right thing to do"

## Key Insight
> "A mission built on bedrock survives failed paths. A mission built on a proxy collapses when the proxy does."
