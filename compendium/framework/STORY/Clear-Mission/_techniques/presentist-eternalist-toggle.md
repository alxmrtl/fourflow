# Presentist-Eternalist Toggle

> A session structure that alternates between two time perspectives — eternalist (wide, directional) for orienting, presentist (narrow, detailed) for executing — creating a rhythm of direction and absorption.

## Mechanism
Presentism (only now is real) and eternalism (all moments coexist) are not philosophical positions but functional modes of attention. Eternalist mode is for navigation: the horizon expands to see the whole arc, past and future coexist, and you can assess trajectory. Presentist mode is for action: the horizon collapses to this moment, this task, this step. Most people get stuck in one mode — either abstractly planning without executing or executing without connecting to direction. The toggle creates a deliberate rhythm between them.

## Protocol

**Session structure:**
1. **Open in eternalist mode (2–3 minutes)**: Review the arc. Where does today's work fit in the larger trajectory? Connect the session to the week, the week to the project, the project to the vision. Don't execute anything yet — just see the map.
2. **Toggle to presentist mode for execution**: Full absorption in the immediate. The horizon collapses to this task, this paragraph, this rep. The session goal (from goal-session-reset) is the only focus. Don't revisit the arc during this phase.
3. **Close with a brief eternalist check (2–3 minutes)**: Does what I did still fit the arc? Did today's work advance the trajectory? Write the session-handoff hook.

**The mechanism**: The opening eternalist mode sets the horizon; the close checks that the horizon was maintained. The session is the presentist phase between these two navigational moments.

**Between sessions**: The gap between work blocks can also use eternalist mode for orientation — reviewing the arc, checking trajectory, calibrating the next session's direction before beginning.

## When to Use
As the default structure for every significant work session. The time investment (5–6 minutes total) is negligible against the navigational value. Also useful when starting a new week (longer eternalist orientation at the beginning).

## Key Insight
> "Eternalism is for navigation. Presentism is for action. The toggle between them IS the practice of outcome horizon setting."
