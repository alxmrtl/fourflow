# Clarity-Importance-Urgency Stack

> The three-ingredient formula for conditions that prevent distraction from penetrating focus — and a diagnostic for identifying which ingredient is missing when engagement collapses.

## Protocol

**The three ingredients:**

1. **Clarity** — The task is specific enough to know exactly what to do next. Must have novelty: challenging enough to require attention, not so routine it runs on autopilot. If overwhelmed, decompose into sub-goals until clarity appears at the sub-goal level.

2. **Importance** — Full understanding of (1) where life ends up without the goal and (2) what becomes possible if the goal is achieved. Importance is not intellectual conviction — it's *felt stakes*. The anti-vision protocol ([[anti-vision-contrast-primer]]) is the practical technique for loading importance.

3. **Urgency** — The felt sense that this must happen now, not eventually. Urgency closes the gap between deciding and acting. Activated by vivid stakes, a defined project, and lever-moving tasks with a clear feedback window.

**Diagnostic use (when engagement collapses):**
- Fuzzy on what to do → **clarity problem**: decompose the task until the next action is trivially clear
- Work feels optional → **importance problem**: revisit the anti-vision; articulate the cost of inaction
- No sense of now → **urgency problem**: identify the one lever-moving task today, set a specific time window

## When to Use
When sitting down to work and noticing low engagement before starting. Mid-session when resistance spikes without clear reason. When a project exists but isn't moving. As a weekly diagnostic on the current project's stack integrity.

## Key Insight
> "Clarity, importance, and urgency are the three critical ingredients that prevent distractions from penetrating. All three are required."
