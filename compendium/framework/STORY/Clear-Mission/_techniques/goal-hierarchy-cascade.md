# Goal Hierarchy Cascade

> A five-level goal structure — 10-year direction down to daily lever-moving tasks — that resolves the paradox of inspiring big goals that don't produce daily clarity.

## Mechanism
Big goals are for direction. Small goals are for clarity. These are different functions and must not be conflated. The 10-year goal tells you which way to walk; the daily task tells you what to do now. Trying to extract daily clarity from a 10-year vision produces paralysis; trying to find directional meaning in a daily task produces emptiness. The cascade bridges them level by level, making each decision at the appropriate scale.

## Protocol

**The five levels:**
1. **10-year goal** — long-range direction; the attractor that orients all other decisions; doesn't need to be precise, needs to be real
2. **1-year goal** — the milestone that makes the 10-year goal feel connected to present action; what would make this year a success toward the decade?
3. **1-month goal** — near enough to feel real, far enough to require strategy; what moves this month that advances the annual goal?
4. **1-week goal** — operational; specific enough to generate clear daily tasks
5. **Daily lever-moving tasks (1–3)** — the specific actions today with the highest causal connection to the weekly goal; not the most urgent, not the most comfortable — the most causally connected to meaningful progress

**The 2-week check:**
After two weeks of completing daily lever-moving tasks, if there's no noticeable progress toward the goal, the tasks were misidentified — they weren't the actual levers. Recalibrate which tasks are causally connected, not just busy-feeling. The check is about direction, not effort.

**The mechanism:** "You don't need endless motivation when the task in front of you is so stupidly simple that you can't help but complete it." Decomposing the hierarchy until daily tasks are trivially clear removes the friction of deciding what to do.

## When to Use
At the start of any new project, season, or major initiative. Weekly to identify the week's goal and that week's lever-moving tasks. When daily work feels disconnected from larger direction (cascade recalibration).

## Key Insight
> "Big goals are for direction. Small goals are for clarity. You are making an educated guess at the path — the 2-week check is how you course-correct without losing direction."
