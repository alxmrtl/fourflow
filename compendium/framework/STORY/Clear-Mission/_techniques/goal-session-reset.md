# Goal Session Reset

> Before each work session (and whenever focus fragments mid-session), write one sentence specifying the single thing you're doing right now — triggering PFC activation and DMN suppression.

## Mechanism
A specific goal doesn't just guide behavior — it reorganizes the brain. Goal-relevant information enters through the prefrontal cortex, triggers dopamine neurons, and creates a self-reinforcing motivation loop. The PFC activates as director of attention, suppressing the DMN's inward-pointing circuitry. Without a specific goal, the brain defaults to the DMN, which runs at the same metabolic cost but points energy toward rumination and anxiety rather than directed effort.

## Protocol

**Before starting a session:**
Write one sentence: *"The one thing I'm doing right now is ___."*

Make it specific enough to know when you're done:
- Not: "work on the project"
- Yes: "draft the opening three paragraphs of the intro section"

Specificity is what gives the PFC something concrete to wire around. Vague goals produce vague neural reorganization.

**Mid-session, when focus fragments:**
Re-state the goal: write or say the same one-sentence format. This re-engages the PFC and re-suppresses DMN drift without requiring a break or reset.

**Session closure:**
Check: did I do the one thing I said? If yes, declare it done. If not, assess whether the drift was from a new goal (legitimate) or from DMN default (a signal for next session).

## When to Use
At the start of every work session, without exception. Whenever focus fragments, distractions penetrate, or the work feels unmotivated mid-session. Before returning to work after any interruption.

## Key Insight
> "Without a goal, your brain isn't resting — it's ruminating. With one, it's building motivation loops."
