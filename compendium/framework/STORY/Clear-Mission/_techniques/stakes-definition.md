# Stakes Definition

> A mission without stated stakes is a task. Name what breaks if it fails and it becomes something worth protecting.

## Protocol
1. Write the mission in one sentence (what you are building or achieving).
2. Complete this sentence: "If this mission fails, what specifically breaks?" Write at least 3 concrete consequences — for yourself, for others, for the future you're trying to build.
3. Rank the consequences by what matters most to you personally. The top item is your primary stake.
4. Write a single stakes statement: "This mission matters because if it doesn't succeed, [primary consequence]. That is not acceptable."
5. Place this statement at the top of your mission document and read it at session start. Not as motivation theater — as an honest account of why the mission has weight.

## When to Use
- When a mission you intellectually believe in feels emotionally flat — you know it matters but can't feel that it does
- Before beginning any project that will span weeks or months and will require sustained effort through difficulty
- When procrastination has become chronic on an important project — often a sign that the stakes haven't been made real

## Key Insight
> "The brain does not protect what it can't feel is at risk."
