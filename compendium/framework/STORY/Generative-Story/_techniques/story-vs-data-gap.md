# Story vs. Data Gap

> What you say your story is and what your behavior reveals are often two different documents — read both.

## Protocol
1. Write your current explicit story in 3–5 sentences: "I am someone who ___, building ___, with a focus on ___."
2. Gather behavioral data for the past 7–14 days. Sources: calendar records, time logs, task completion data, actual output. Don't estimate — look at what actually happened.
3. From the behavioral data, infer the revealed story: "If I only knew what this person did — not what they said — what would I conclude their story is?"
4. Compare the two stories side by side. List every divergence.
5. For each divergence, decide: does the explicit story need to update (it was aspirational, not real) or does the behavior need to change (the story is right but not yet enacted)?
6. Rewrite the explicit story to reflect only what is currently being enacted, with clear annotations for what you intend to move toward.

## When to Use
- During quarterly or monthly reviews when you want honest signal on whether stated priorities are matching actual behavior
- When progress feels inexplicably slow despite believing you're working hard
- When someone close to you reflects back a version of you that doesn't match your self-image — use this to determine who's right

## Key Insight
> "Behavior is an honest narrator. It doesn't have a PR team."
