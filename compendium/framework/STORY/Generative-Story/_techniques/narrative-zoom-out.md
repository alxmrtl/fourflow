# Narrative Zoom Out

> Present friction is loud but small — zoom out and the story reappears.

## Mechanism
Attention is perceptually dominated by what is closest in time. Current difficulty fills the attentional frame not because it is the most important thing in the arc but because it is the most immediate. This is an evolutionary feature — the brain prioritizes proximal threats — but it produces systematic distortion: present friction reads as catastrophic when it occupies the full frame.

Deliberately shifting to a longer time horizon (5-year view) reorganizes the spatial metaphor the brain uses to represent significance. The hippocampus and prefrontal cortex co-construct a mental timeline; when you extend the visible horizon, the relative size of current friction shrinks without the friction changing. The threat signal attenuates because the larger story context reinstates the proportion between this moment and the whole.

There is also a motivational mechanism: the longer horizon surfaces goals and outcomes that are not yet visible at the session level. These future states act as attractor points — the brain's forward-projection systems (default mode network, prospection) reactivate and reestablish narrative pull, which is experienced as renewed motivation.

## Protocol
1. Stop the current task. Set a 3-minute timer.
2. Ask: "What is the 5-year view of what I'm building?" Write the answer in 3–5 sentences — not a plan, a story. What exists in 5 years that doesn't exist today?
3. Now locate the current friction in that story: "In the 5-year story, this moment is ___." (It will be small — a week, a paragraph, an obstacle that was solved.)
4. Note the proportion explicitly: "This moment is [fraction] of the total arc."
5. Return to the work with the proportion active. The friction hasn't changed; its meaning has.

## When to Use
- When a single difficult day or session has started to color the entire project negatively
- When present difficulty feels disproportionately significant ("this is ruining everything")
- After a setback that has caused re-evaluation of whether the mission is worth continuing

## Key Insight
> "Zoom level determines meaning. What looks like a wall at street level is a single brick from altitude."
