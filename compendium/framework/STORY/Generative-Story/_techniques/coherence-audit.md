# Coherence Audit

> A periodic three-activity check to detect meaning coherence leaks — places where your actions are telling different stories instead of one.

## Protocol

**Weekly or biweekly:**

1. List your three primary activities this week (where you spent the most time/energy).
2. For each, complete the sentence: "This action tells the story that I am someone who ___."
3. Compare the three stories. Do they fit one frame, or three separate ones?
4. Where they diverge: **coherence leak identified.**

**For each leak, choose one:**
- **Reframe**: find the interpretation that makes this activity part of the larger story. Sometimes the activity is aligned but the story you're telling about it is off. Reframing closes the leak without changing the activity.
- **Recognize it doesn't belong**: the activity is genuinely disconnected from your current frame and is costing subjective time at a rate that's not worth the return. This signals renegotiation or elimination.

**The goal is one story, not zero activities.** The aim isn't to eliminate things — it's to be deliberate enough that everything connects.

## When to Use
Weekly or biweekly as a standing audit. When time feels choppy or fragmented despite an apparently full schedule. When motivation is inconsistent across different areas of work. When a new project or commitment has been added and its fit to the frame is unclear.

## Key Insight
> "Meaning coherence doesn't just make work feel purposeful — it makes time feel continuous. Incoherence fragments the aperture into disconnected moments."
