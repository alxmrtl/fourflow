# Disconfirmation Check

> A story that only admits supporting evidence isn't a story — it's a fortress.

## Protocol
1. State your current operative story in one sentence: "My story is that I am ___" or "My story is that this project is ___."
2. Ask: "What evidence exists that this story is wrong or incomplete?" Spend at minimum 3 minutes generating this list — do not stop at one or two items.
3. For each piece of disconfirming evidence: does it change the story, modify it, or get genuinely explained by the story? Be honest about which is which.
4. Update the story: write a revised version that accounts for both confirming and disconfirming evidence. It will be less clean and more accurate.
5. Run this check weekly or whenever the story has been unchallenged for more than two weeks.

## When to Use
- When a story about yourself, your project, or your progress has been held unchallenged for a long time and feels very certain
- Before making a major decision based on a current narrative (e.g., "I'm ready to launch," "This approach is working")
- When you notice you've been explaining away negative signals rather than integrating them

## Key Insight
> "The stories that most need checking are the ones that feel most obviously true."
