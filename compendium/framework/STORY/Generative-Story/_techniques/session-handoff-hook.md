# Session Handoff Hook

> At the end of each work session, write one sentence: "Tomorrow I pick up at ___." — creating an open narrative loop that pulls re-entry without willpower.

## Protocol

**At the end of every work session**, before closing anything:

Write one sentence in this format: *"Tomorrow I pick up at ___."*

Make it specific enough to be immediately actionable:
- Not: "Tomorrow I continue the project"
- Yes: "Tomorrow I pick up at the second argument in section 3, after outlining the counterpoint"

**Why one sentence:** The specificity isn't for planning — it's for the open loop. The more specific the thread, the stronger the pull back into it. A vague hook creates a vague pull; a specific hook creates the sensation of a story already in progress.

**Keep the hook visible:** Write it where you'll see it at the start of the next session — the top of the next day's notes, a sticky note on the screen, the first line of tomorrow's document.

**The mirror delay framing:** When you've made an internal shift but external reality hasn't caught up, use the hook to reframe the gap as narrative: "Tomorrow I pick up at the next chapter of this arc." The gap between sessions becomes the space where character is built, not empty time to dread.

## When to Use
At the end of every significant work session, as a non-negotiable closing practice. Especially critical for long-horizon projects where daily momentum is the primary motivational resource. Also useful when returning to work after breaks, illness, or disruption — write the hook immediately to re-establish the thread.

## Key Insight
> "Time is the sensation of your trajectory. When you stop moving through your story, time stops feeling like yours. The hook keeps the story in motion between sessions."
