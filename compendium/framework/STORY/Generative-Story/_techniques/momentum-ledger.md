# Momentum Ledger

> Momentum is invisible without a record — the ledger converts movement into evidence you can see.

## Protocol
1. Keep a dedicated document or physical notebook — one entry per work day. Title format: date + one word for the day's theme (e.g., "Mar 27 — structure").
2. At session end, write exactly three entries:
   - **Moved**: what actually advanced today (even one sentence)
   - **Unlocked**: what became possible because of today's movement
   - **Next thread**: what's the living edge right now (feeds into the Incomplete Open Thread technique)
3. Review the last 7 entries every Monday before starting work. Not to evaluate — to see the arc. Ask only: "Is this moving?"
4. When momentum feels absent, read back 30 days. Absence of momentum is rarely as total as it feels.

## When to Use
- On any project spanning more than two weeks where daily output isn't obviously visible
- When motivation has been inconsistent and the narrative of "nothing is happening" has taken hold
- As a standing daily practice during any sustained creative or building phase

## Key Insight
> "You can't feel momentum — you can only see it. The ledger is the instrument."
