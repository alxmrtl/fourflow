# Meaning Coherence

> Ensures actions fit a unified frame.

## How It Enables Flow

Meaning coherence is the condition where your actions, your narrative, and your deepest values all point in the same direction. When coherent, every action reinforces the story, every story expresses the values, and the whole system operates as one signal. Attention doesn't fragment because there's nothing competing — the frame holds.

When incoherent, you're doing things that don't connect to what you say matters, or telling a story that doesn't match what you actually do. The system splits. Part of your processing monitors the gap ("why am I doing this?"), part manages the dissonance ("it'll make sense eventually"), and part leaks energy into justification. Flow becomes expensive because the aperture is partially occluded by internal contradiction.

The connection to time: meaning coherence determines how you experience duration. When actions fit a unified frame, time compresses — hours feel like minutes because every moment is part of one continuous signal. When actions don't fit, time fragments — each task feels like a separate effort, disconnected from the last, and the subjective cost of each moment increases.

## Techniques & Applications

### Stabilizing the Light (Source: What Time Actually Is)

Time as aperture means your consciousness can only illuminate one cross-section of reality at a time. Meaning coherence is what keeps the light aimed in a consistent direction — so each successive cross-section builds on the last rather than jumping to unrelated slices.

When your actions fit a unified frame, the light sweeps a coherent path through the structure. Each moment illuminates a section adjacent to the last. This is why coherent work feels like flow — you're seeing a continuous painting, not random frames. When meaning is incoherent, the light jumps — illuminating work that doesn't connect to relationships that don't connect to values. Each jump costs a perceptual reset. Time feels choppy because you're generating discontinuity.

**Practice — The Coherence Audit**:
1. List your three primary activities this week (where you spent the most time).
2. For each, answer: "What story does this action tell about who I am?"
3. Compare the three stories. Do they fit one frame or three separate ones?
4. Where they diverge, you've found a coherence leak — a place where your light is scattering rather than sweeping a unified path.

**What to do with leaks**: You don't necessarily eliminate activities. You reframe them within the larger arc, or you recognize that one of them genuinely doesn't belong and is costing you more subjective time than it's worth. The goal is one story, not three.

**Key insight**: Meaning coherence doesn't just make work feel purposeful — it makes time feel continuous. Incoherence fragments the aperture into disconnected moments.

### The Faith-Mirror Coherence Test (Source: What Time Actually Is)

The "mirror" model says external reality reflects internal state, but with a delay. Meaning coherence is what you hold steady across that delay. If your internal frame shifts every time the mirror doesn't update fast enough (doubt, pivoting, abandoning direction), coherence breaks — and with it, the narrative momentum that would have carried you to the reflection.

**The test**: When external reality isn't confirming your direction, do you:
- (a) Hold the frame and keep acting from it — meaning stays coherent
- (b) Start hedging, questioning the direction, splitting attention — meaning fragments

Option (b) is the most common way meaning coherence breaks. Not through dramatic crisis but through quiet erosion — small doubts accumulating until the unified frame cracks.

**Practice**: When you notice hedging or doubt:
1. Ask: "Has my internal direction actually changed, or has the mirror just not caught up?"
2. If your direction hasn't changed, the doubt is noise. Re-commit to the frame.
3. If your direction has genuinely shifted, update the frame cleanly rather than running two stories simultaneously.

**Key insight**: Faith isn't a spiritual luxury — it's the mechanism that maintains meaning coherence across the gap between intention and evidence.
