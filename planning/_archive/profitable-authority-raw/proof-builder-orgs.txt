1. OFFER

Team Conditions Audit — a 90-minute diagnostic session for people leaders,
founders, and team managers.

Deliverable: a named team bottleneck, a targeted first intervention, one
observable success indicator, and a one-page written Team Conditions Map.

The diagnostic uses the FourFlow framework, which maps team conditions across
four layers — Self, Space, Story, Spirit — to identify which specific condition
is blocking the team's best work.

Larger offer behind it: a full diagnostic engagement (one week to 30 days)
and a 6-week conditions restoration program. Both currently in design.


---

2. BEST CLIENT

People leaders, team managers, and founders of 5–500 person knowledge-work
organizations — tech, consulting, creative, professional services — who have
a capable team that is not producing at the level the talent should allow.

Specifically: someone who has already invested in at least one engagement,
wellness, or culture initiative and found that the output gap did not close.

They have budget, authority to change conditions, and enough self-awareness
to know their current diagnosis is probably incomplete.


---

3. PAINFUL PROBLEM

They are leading a team that looks functional but is underperforming its
potential. Work ships. Nobody is in crisis. But the output is flat, energy
is inconsistent, strong people are quietly disengaging or leaving, and
previous engagement programs produced no lasting change.

The precise pain: they cannot name which condition is actually creating the
friction. Without a diagnosis, they are choosing between vague interventions.
They are about to run another program they are not confident will work.


---

4. OUTCOME

After the Team Conditions Audit, the leader can answer three questions:

Which condition is blocking the team's best work?
What do we change first?
How will we know if the change worked?

The concrete deliverable: a named bottleneck, a targeted action, a 30-day
success indicator, and a one-page written map.

The 30-day marker: a visible shift in work quality, energy, ownership, or
retention risk — not survey scores, actual observable change in the work.

The longer-term transformation: conditions actively managed instead of
passively assumed. The leader can detect friction building before it becomes
disengagement, burnout, or resignation.

6. BEFORE/AFTER

Personal / self-diagnostic (the origin story):

Before: Working inside an organizational structure, aware of the gap between
capability and output, unable to name the cause with enough precision to close
it. Trying standard approaches — routines, environment changes, mindset work —
finding they helped in isolation but did not address the actual pattern.

After: Built a diagnostic framework that named the specific conditions creating
friction. Applied it systematically. The gap between capability and output
became manageable — not through willpower or new tools, but through identifying
and restoring the specific conditions that were blocked.

This is the founding story of FourFlow and the credibility of the lived experience
angle.

/align sessions (prototype diagnostic in action):

Before each session: a stated situation, a felt state, a set of conditions in
play across the four pillars. Some sessions show clearly blocked conditions —
narrative friction, values misalignment, environment creating drag.

After each session: a named condition, a specific recommendation, and in many
cases a documented change in approach based on the diagnosis.

These sessions exist as session logs in `agents/flow-council/sessions/`.
They are the most direct before/after evidence available.

No formal before/after yet. First paid sessions will generate this.

11. BUYER DOUBTS

What a skeptical buyer might doubt:

"Who is this person in an organizational context?"
The framework was built personally and tested personally. There is no prior
employer, consulting firm, or client roster that places this in a recognizable
professional category. The offer may read as a solo practitioner with a
personal-development framework, not an organizational diagnostic specialist.

"Has this actually worked with a team?"
The lived experience is real. The organizational case study is not yet built.
A skeptical buyer will notice this gap if they look.

"The framework sounds interesting but I'm not sure it's different from what
we've already tried."
Story and Spirit are the differentiated layers. But a buyer scanning the
offer quickly may not see the distinction from generic culture or engagement
work without a specific before/after that shows what changes.

"Is this just flow coaching rebranded as organizational consulting?"
The word "flow" carries personal-development associations that may create
category confusion. The buyer needs to hear this as a business diagnostic
offer, not a consciousness or peak-performance coaching offer.

"Why would 90 minutes be enough to diagnose something that has been unresolved
for months?"
The answer is that most organizational problems are already visible in the
conditions — they have just not been mapped. But this doubt is real and
needs to be addressed directly in the positioning.

"What do I do with the one-page map after I have it?"
The offer stops at the diagnosis and the first action. Some buyers will want
to know there is a path beyond the 90-minute session. The delivery ladder
(one week, 30 days, 6 weeks) answers this, but it should be easy to find.

13. ETHICAL LIMITS

Claims to avoid until the organizational case study exists:

"I've helped teams increase retention / output / engagement by X%."
Not yet supportable. Do not imply a specific quantitative result.

"My clients have seen [specific result]."
No organizational clients yet. Cannot use "clients" language for the
Team Conditions Audit specifically until the first paid session has run.

"Proven framework."
Proven for personal application and personal flow diagnostics. Not yet proven
for team-level organizational diagnosis in a paid engagement context. Use
"developed" or "built from" instead.

"Transform your team's performance."
Too strong without a case study behind it. "Identify what is blocking your
team's best work" is what can be promised and delivered.

Claims that are fully supportable:

"Built from [X] years of personal application."
"Grounded in research across [named fields]."
"A diagnostic that names the condition before prescribing the fix."
"Most engagement programs are deployed before diagnosis — this one starts there."
"In 90 minutes you will have a named bottleneck, a targeted action, and a
way to tell if it is working."
