## Quality Principles for New Files

When creating new technique or concept files, hold this standard:

**For techniques:**
- Is it atomic? (one protocol, not a mix of protocols)
- Is it specific enough to follow without reading context?
- Does it address a state that actually blocks flow?
- Is it different from every other technique already in the same quality?

**For concepts:**
- Is this genuinely external (not FourFlow-proprietary)?
- Does it explain WHY the quality works, not just rephrase it?
- Is it the most essential framework for this quality, not just a related one?
- Would a skeptic use this to validate the quality's existence?

**Anti-patterns to avoid:**
- Padding to hit the number (content that doesn't add new leverage)
- Near-duplicates with slightly different names
- Concepts that are just definitions, not frameworks with mechanism
- Techniques that require context to execute (not truly atomic)
