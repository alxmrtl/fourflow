# Internal Alignment

> Removes value-based friction.

## How It Enables Flow

When there's misalignment between what you're doing and your deeper truth, friction arises. Part of your energy goes toward suppressing the misalignment rather than engaging fully with the task. This creates the subtle background tension that blocks flow - you're fighting yourself while trying to work.

Internal alignment means your actions, emotions, and authentic desires are congruent. There's no part of you pulling in a different direction. This removes the internal friction that fragments attention and energy. When aligned, the whole system moves together - mind, heart, body, and deeper self all oriented toward the same direction.

The deeper insight: there's an intelligence in life that responds to your internal integrity. When you're aligned with your authentic self, circumstances tend to flow more easily. When you're misaligned - acting from patterns rather than truth - you encounter more friction as life seems to push back. This isn't magical thinking; it's pattern recognition about how alignment and ease correlate.

## Techniques & Applications

### Internal Integrity Check (Source: Conscious Accomplishment)

Internal integrity is a measure of whether you're aligned with the emotions, sensations, and authentic desires in your inner space. It's the degree to which your external actions match your internal truth.

**High internal integrity looks like**:
- Actions feel natural and easeful (even when challenging)
- No suppressed voice saying "this isn't right"
- Energy flows toward the work rather than managing discomfort
- Outcomes matter less because the process itself feels aligned

**Low internal integrity looks like**:
- Persistent tension or resistance during action
- Rationalizing why you're doing something
- Energy leaking into suppression, performance, or forcing
- Needing outcomes to justify the misalignment

**Practice**: Before and during significant work, check: "Am I aligned with what I'm doing, or am I overriding something?" This isn't about always feeling good - challenge is fine. The question is whether the challenge comes from growth or from betraying your truth.

### Life's Intelligence and Ease (Source: Conscious Accomplishment)

Life's intelligence is the underlying force that animates and orchestrates experience. This view holds that reality is responsive - there's a larger intelligence at work, and you can align with or resist its direction.

**Three principles of life's intelligence**:
1. **Encourages your evolution**: Circumstances arise that aid awareness and transformation
2. **Rewards internal integrity**: When you're aligned with your authentic self, things tend to flow
3. **Has a natural direction**: There's a way things want to unfold that supports your growth and contribution

**Discerning direction**: Notice ease vs. friction as you move toward things. This isn't "avoid hard things" - growth involves challenge. But there's a difference between the difficulty of stretching and the friction of forcing something misaligned. Ease within effort signals alignment; persistent friction despite effort signals misalignment.

**Practice**: When facing a decision, instead of only analyzing pros/cons, notice: "Does moving toward this feel like ease or friction in my body?" The body often knows alignment before the mind figures it out.

### Authentic Desires vs. Conditioned Wants (Source: Conscious Accomplishment)

Not all desires are equal. Some emerge from your authentic nature; others are programmed by conditioning. Learning to distinguish them is key to internal alignment.

**Conditioned wants** often have a "should" quality:
- "I should want this because it's successful"
- "I want this so people will think X about me"
- Driven by comparison, approval, or escape from unworthiness
- Feel heavy, obligatory, or never-quite-satisfied

**Authentic desires** have different qualities:
- Arise without needing justification
- Feel enlivening rather than burdensome
- Not about proving or escaping anything
- The journey itself has value, not just arrival

**How to work with this**: Accept and embrace your desires fully - even the conditioned ones. Denial creates inefficiency and blocks self-awareness. Full acceptance lets you see clearly what's driving you. From that clarity, you can work with patterns while honoring what's authentic.

**Why this matters for flow**: Pursuing conditioned wants puts you at war with yourself. The pattern drives action while the deeper self resists. This split attention makes flow impossible. Authentic desires align the system - you move with yourself rather than against.

### The Pushed vs. Pulled Assessment (Source: Conscious Accomplishment)

A practical diagnostic for alignment: Are you being pushed toward something or pulled?

**Pushed** (often misaligned):
- Action driven by fear, obligation, or escape
- Feels like forcing, efforting, grinding
- Motivation requires external pressure or willpower
- "I have to do this or else..."

**Pulled** (often aligned):
- Action drawn by interest, curiosity, or meaning
- Feels like engagement, even when hard
- Motivation arises naturally
- "I want to see where this goes..."

**Practice**: For any significant project or goal, ask: "Am I being pushed or pulled here?" If pushed, explore what pattern is driving it. If pulled, lean in. This doesn't mean avoid all pushed work - sometimes necessity requires it. But knowing the difference helps you understand where you're aligned and where you're overriding.

### Heart-True Self Connection Protocol (Source: Conscious Accomplishment)

The heart is the portal to the True Self (SOUL), and the True Self is the source of authentic wisdom about alignment. Strengthening this connection improves your access to guidance about what's truly aligned.

**The protocol** (can be done briefly or extended):
1. Find a calm, settled state through breath (I-am-here-now)
2. Shift attention to heart space in the chest
3. Connect through intention and feeling - sense the heart's qualities
4. Invite connection to the True Self/SOUL
5. From this unified state, pose your question about alignment
6. Remain open to what emerges - feeling, voice, image, knowing

**Why it works**: The thinking mind operates from patterns and conditioning. The True Self sees beyond these. By establishing the connection first, you access wisdom that the thinking mind alone can't provide. The answer may come as a direct knowing rather than a logical analysis.
