# Vision Priming

> Preloads direction before action begins.

## How It Enables Flow

Vision provides the long-range attractor that orients action. Without direction, attention scatters - you don't know what to engage with, so everything competes equally. With vision primed, the system has a north star that makes moment-to-moment decisions effortless. Relevant opportunities become obvious; irrelevant distractions fade.

But there's a trap: grasping too hard for a vision can itself become a source of disturbance. The pressure to "find your purpose" creates anxiety that closes the heart and obscures the very clarity you're seeking. Vision priming works best when it emerges naturally from a cleared lens rather than being forced through effort.

## Techniques & Applications

### Living Into Purpose (Source: Conscious Accomplishment)

Conscious accomplishment views discovering and living into deeper purpose as a *function of consciousness evolution*. Purpose isn't something you find through analysis or force of will - it emerges naturally as you clear the patterns that obscure it.

**The purpose gap**: The distance between where you are and your deeper purpose isn't primarily about information or strategy. It's about the impediments - patterns, beliefs, fears - that obscure your vision and prevent you from stepping into it easily.

**Closing the gap**: Work on the patterns blocking clarity rather than pushing harder to "figure out" your purpose. Each pattern transformed clears the lens further. What you couldn't see before becomes visible. The direction that felt vague becomes specific. Purpose reveals itself through the cleared aperture.

**Why this takes pressure off**: For some, a singular life purpose or vision may not be immediately attainable - and that's fine. The framework suggests that re-patterning and clearing disturbances should take *priority* over needing to "find" an exact purpose right away. Purpose is downstream of clarity. Clear the lens first; vision follows.

**The spiral view**: Rather than climbing a "mountain of success" toward a fixed destination, conscious accomplishment suggests a "spiral of fulfillment" where each cycle of inner work reveals more of your authentic direction. Purpose deepens and refines as you evolve. It's not static.

**Practice**: When feeling lost about direction:
1. Notice the anxiety or pressure around "not having a purpose"
2. Recognize this as pattern material - take it into Re-Patterning
3. Transform the need to know before you can act
4. From the cleared state, notice what naturally draws your attention
5. Follow that thread without needing it to be "the answer"

The purpose that emerges from a clear heart and open mind is more authentic than anything manufactured under pressure.
